## Description

A peaceful walking route starting from the Upper Macleay Park Trailhead, following the paved Lower Macleay Trail and then transitioning onto the Wildwood Trail deep into Forest Park's old-growth forest. The route climbs gently through towering Douglas firs and western red cedars, crossing small streams along the way, and ends at the historic Witch's Castle (Stone House) on the Wildwood Trail — a perfect photo stop and rest point.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start** — Upper Macleay Park Trailhead (NW 29th Ave & Skyline Blvd, 45.5270, -122.7275)
2. **Head east** on the paved Lower Macleay Trail, following the well-marked path through the parking area
3. **Continue** on the trail as it transitions from pavement to packed dirt — entering the forest canopy after ~400 m
4. **Follow** the Wildwood Trail northbound — the trail winds gently uphill through old-growth forest with occasional creek crossings
5. **Pass** several trail junctions — stay on the main Wildwood Trail (left at any unmarked intersections)
6. **Arrive** at the Witch's Castle (Stone House) — a relic stone structure from the 1890s, a great rest spot and photo opportunity (~2.0 km from trailhead)
7. **Optional continue** — the Wildwood Trail continues north toward the park's interior if you want to extend the walk
8. **Return** by retracing your steps back to the Upper Macleay Trailhead

## Amenities Nearby

- Parking at Upper Macleay Trailhead (small lot on NW 29th Ave)
- No restrooms at trailhead — use facilities at Macleay Park entrance (~400 m south)
- No water on trail — bring your own
- Nearby: The Roads Less Traveled café on NW 29th Ave

## Tips

- Trail can be slippery after rain — wear shoes with good grip
- The Witch's Castle is a popular stop — the group can meet there for photos
- Best in morning to avoid afternoon fog in the forest
- Watch for trail runners using the Wildwood Trail
- Leashed dogs are permitted on this trail
- The last ~300 m of trail toward Witch's Castle has some rocky sections — take care
