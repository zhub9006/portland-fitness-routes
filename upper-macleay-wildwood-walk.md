## Description

A gentle, scenic walk from the Upper Macleay Trailhead up the Wildwood Trail to the historic Stone House area. This short but rewarding trail winds through dense old-growth forest with towering Douglas firs, Western red cedars, and bigleaf maples. The trail crosses small streams and passes through thick understory before reaching the Stone House — a moss-covered stone structure that's a favorite landmark for the running club. Ideal for beginners, families, or as a warm-up before longer routes.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start** — Upper Macleay Park Trailhead (NW 29th Ave at Skyline Blvd)
2. **Head south** on the Wildwood Trail — packed dirt single-track, well-marked
3. **Descend gently** through old-growth forest — ~400 m of shaded trail
4. **Cross** NW Cornell Road at the trail junction (use caution, low traffic)
5. **Continue** south on the Wildwood Trail for ~600 m
6. **Arrive** at the Stone House (Witch's Castle) — a historic stone ruin surrounded by moss and ferns
7. **Explore** the Stone House area, then retrace your steps back to the trailhead

## Amenities Nearby

- Parking at Upper Macleay Trailhead
- Bus stop on NW Skyline Blvd (TriMet routes)
- No water on trail — bring your own
- Nearby: The Anchor café, Pilot House café

## Tips

- The trail is shaded year-round — great for hot summer days
- Can be slippery near stream crossings after rain
- The Stone House is an OSU heritage site — please don't climb on it
- Leashed dogs welcome
- Popular with trail runners — be prepared to share the trail on weekends
- Best visited in morning for optimal lighting and fewer crowds
