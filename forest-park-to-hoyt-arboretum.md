## Description

A pleasant walking route connecting two of Portland's natural treasures: Hoyt Arboretum and Forest Park. Starting from the Hoyt Arboretum Visitor Center, the route follows shaded neighborhood streets and the Sigman Creek Trail into the southern edge of Forest Park. This is an ideal route for a group warm-up walk or a combined arboretum-to-forest excursion.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start** — Hoyt Arboretum Visitor Center (SW Military Ln & Arboretum Dr, 45.5158, -122.7165)
2. **Head north** on Arboretum Drive through the Hoyt Arboretum grounds — enjoy the tree collections on both sides (~400 m)
3. **Exit** the arboretum at the north end onto SW Berrywood Dr, turning left (west)
4. **Walk** along SW Berrywood Dr for ~300 m, crossing SW Fairview Blvd
5. **Turn right** (north) onto SW Fairview Blvd, using the sidewalk — ~500 m gentle climb
6. **Continue** north on SW Fairview Blvd to NW Savier St, then turn left (west)
7. **Walk** west on NW Savier St for ~350 m, then turn right (north) onto NW 25th Ave
8. **Follow** NW 25th Ave north for ~400 m, then turn left (west) onto NW Lovejoy St
9. **Walk** west on NW Lovejoy St for ~200 m, arriving at the Forest Park boundary at NW Cornell Road
10. **Enter** Forest Park via the pedestrian trail entrance on NW Cornell Road — the route transitions to a packed dirt/gravel trail
11. **Follow** the interior trail north-northwest for ~300 m through the forest edge
12. **Arrive** at the Forest Park interior trail network — connect to the main park trails

## Amenities Nearby

- Parking at Hoyt Arboretum (free, visitor center lot)
- Restrooms and water at Hoyt Arboretum Visitor Center
- No water in Forest Park interior — bring your own
- Nearby: SW Moody & Co. Café near the arboretum exit

## Tips

- The Hoyt Arboretum section is flat and paved — great for easy walking
- The transition to Forest Park trails involves a short road walk (~1 km of quiet neighborhood streets)
- Trails inside Forest Park can be muddy after rain — wear appropriate shoes
- Best in morning — afternoon fog can reduce visibility in the forest
- Combine with a visit to the arboretum for a full nature outing
- This route is beginner-friendly and suitable for all fitness levels
