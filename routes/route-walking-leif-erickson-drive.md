# Route: Leif Erikson Drive Walk (Easy) 🟢

## Overview
| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Walking |
| **Difficulty** | Easy |
| **Distance** | ~5 miles (10 miles round trip) |
| **Elevation Gain** | ~400 ft |
| **Terrain** | Paved road, well-maintained |
| **Surface** | Asphalt (car-free on weekdays & Sundays) |
| **Start** | Lower Macleay Park entrance (NW Cornell Rd & NW 29th Ave) |
| **End** | Hoyt Arboretum / Upper Macleay area |

## Description
Leif Erikson Drive is the main car-free road running through Forest Park and the most popular route for walkers and runners. This out-and-back covers the scenic lower section from Lower Macleay Park to the Hoyt Arboretum area, passing through beautiful old-growth forest with minimal elevation gain.

## Route Description
1. Start at Lower Macleay Park entrance (NW Cornell Rd & NW 29th Ave)
2. Head north on Leif Erikson Drive, a paved road closed to cars
3. Pass the Wildwood Trail intersection on your right
4. Continue through dense forest canopy with tall firs and maples
5. Arrive at the Hoyt Arboretum area — a great spot for a rest stop
6. Turn around and return the same way for an out-and-back walk

## Key Points
- **Lower Macleay Park:** Open meadow near the start, popular for warm-ups
- **Wildwood Trail Intersection:** Major trail junction — note the trailhead sign
- **Hoyt Arboretum:** Botanical collection of trees from around the world
- **Forest Viewpoints:** Several spots with views of the Portland skyline and Mt. Hood on clear days

## OSM Route Context
**Coordinates:** 45.5358° N, 122.7386° W (Forest Park center)
**Primary Area:** Leif Erikson Drive corridor, NW 29th Ave to Hoyt Arboretum
**Nearby Roads:** NW 29th Ave (main access), NW Cornell Rd

**OSM Notes:** Leif Erikson Drive is mapped as a `highway=path` in OSM within Forest Park. It is functional as a car-free road for pedestrian and cyclist use.

## Best For
- Beginners and casual walkers
- Dog walkers (leash recommended)
- Family outings
- Post-work evening walks (well-lit in lower sections)

## Trail Conditions & Preparedness
| Condition | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Dry | Comfortable walking shoes are fine |
| Wet | Watch for slippery leaves on pavement |
| Winter | Road may have ice patches — use caution |

### What to Bring
- **Water:** Fountain available at Lower Macleay Park
- **Layers:** Forest canopy keeps it cooler — light jacket recommended
- **Sunscreen:** Open sections near the arboretum can be sunny
- **Emergency:** Phone, ID

## Seasonal Tips
- **Spring:** Beautiful wildflowers along the trail edges
- **Summer:** Dense canopy provides natural shade
- **Fall:** Spectacular foliage colors in the lower sections
- **Winter:** Shorter daylight — bring a headlamp for evening walks
