## Description
The Wildwood Trail is Portland's premier trail running route, winding through 5,200 acres of old-growth forest in Forest Park. This loop combines the Wildwood Trail with the Lower Macleay Trail for a scenic forest running experience with moderate elevation gain.

## Route Description
1. Start at Lower Macleay Trailhead (NW 29th Ave & NW Upshur St)
2. Follow the Wildwood Trail north through dense old-growth forest
3. Pass through the Lower Macleay Park area with its open meadow
4. Continue on the Wildwood Trail as it winds through the forest canopy
5. Return via the Lower Macleay Trail back to the trailhead

## Key Points
- **Wildwood Trail:** The main trail running through the park — ~30 miles of trail total
- **Lower Macleay Meadow:** Open area near the start, good for warm-up/cool-down
- **Forest Park Visitor Center:** Located near the Stuart-Streets Trailhead area
- **Hoyt Arboretum:** Pass by the arboretum on the lower loop
- **Redwood Trail:** Connects to old-growth redwood groves

## Trail Conditions & Preparedness
| Condition | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Dry | Standard running shoes work well |
| Wet/Muddy | Trail running shoes with aggressive tread |
| Post-rain | Expect roots and rocks to be slippery — slower pace |

### What to Bring
- **Water:** No water fountains on trail — carry your own (minimum 1L)
- **Footwear:** Trail shoes recommended over road shoes
- **Layers:** Forest canopy keeps it cooler — light moisture-wicking top
- **Emergency:** Phone, ID, small first aid kit
- **Map:** Download offline map — cell service is spotty in deep forest

## Best Times to Run
- **Morning (6-9 AM):** Cooler temps, fewer people, best trail conditions
- **Weekday Evenings (5-7 PM):** Less crowded than weekends
- **Avoid:** Midday in summer (hot under canopy), after heavy rain (muddy)

## Trail Etiquette
- Yield to pedestrians and hikers
- Keep right, pass on the left
- Ring bell or call "On your left!" when passing
- Stay on marked trails — no cutting switchbacks
- Pack out all trash

## Training Value
- **Cadence:** Natural trail forces shorter stride → higher cadence
- **Strength:** Roots and rocks improve balance and ankle strength
- **Mental:** Forest immersion reduces perceived exertion → harder effort feels easier
- **Cross-Training:** Excellent complement to road running for injury prevention
