# Lower Macleay → Pittock Mansion Walking Route

**Type:** Walking/Hiking
**OSM Verified Distance:** 4,923 m
**Estimated Duration:** ~59 min walking (5 km/h)
**Elevation Gain:** ~550 ft
**Difficulty:** Moderate

---

## Route Overview

This is the classic Forest Park out-and-back hike. Starting from Lower Macleay Park and climbing up through the dense forest to the Pittock Mansion, this route offers deep forest immersion followed by expansive city views at the summit.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions (OSM Verified)

1. **Start:** Lower Macleay Park trailhead (45.534, -122.713)
2. Head northwest on **Lower Macleay Trail** (~392 m)
3. Turn left onto **NW Thurman Street** (~853 m) — residential street, well-paved
4. Turn right onto **NW 25th Avenue** (~392 m)
5. Continue on **NW 25th Avenue** (~386 m total)
6. Turn left onto **NW Westover Road** (~373 m)
7. Turn right onto **NW 24th Place** (~176 m)
8. Turn left onto **West Burnside Street** (~483 m)
9. Continue on **West Burnside Road** (~1,130 m) — this begins the climb
10. Turn right onto **NW Barnes Road** (~174 m)
11. Turn right onto **NW Pittock Avenue** (~280 m)
12. Continue onto **NW Pittock Drive** (~455 m) — the final approach to the mansion
13. **Arrive:** Pittock Mansion (45.525, -122.716)

## Seasonal Notes

- **Spring:** Wildflowers along the trail; muddy sections near creek crossings
- **Summer:** Dense canopy; bring water — limited shade on Burnside Road section
- **Fall:** Spectacular foliage in the Pittock Drive section
- **Winter:** Can be icy on the Burnside Road hill; microspikes recommended

## Tips

- The hike up is harder than the walk down; allow extra time
- Pittock Mansion grounds are free to explore; museum entry has a small fee
- Mountain bikes can ride the West Burnside Road/Portland section but not the trail
- Pack layers — the mansion is 500 ft higher than the trailhead
