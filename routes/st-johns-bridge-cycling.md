# St. Johns Bridge Cycling Route (Detailed)

Full turn-by-turn cycling route from Forest Park through neighborhoods to the St. Johns Bridge.

## Route Overview

- **Type:** Cycling
- **Total Distance:** 14,746 m one way
- **Elevation Gain:** ~800 ft (ascent to St. Johns)
- **Difficulty:** Challenging (climb to St. Johns!)
- **Surface:** Road; mix of neighborhoods and park roads

---

## Route Description

This detailed cycling route goes from the heart of Forest Park north through residential NW Portland neighborhoods, climbing steadily to the iconic St. Johns Bridge. It's a challenging ascent but the views from the bridge top it all off.

### Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start at Forest Park (45.571, -122.772)**
   - NW Saint Helens Road area

2. **Head south on NW Saint Helens Road** (3,072 m)
   - Through Forest Park
   - Smartly shaded and peaceful

3. **Continue through the Hollow and transition streets** (various)
   - Leave the forest and enter residential NW

4. **West on NW Bridge Avenue** (958 m)
   - Approach the St. Johns Bridge

5. **Cross the St. Johns Bridge** (1,093 m)
   - Iconic suspension bridge!
   - Stunning panoramic views from the top
   - Watch for wind and gusts

6. **Continue to St. Johns** (various segments)
   - NW Philadelphia Avenue (104 m)
   - NW Willamette Boulevard (179 m)
   - NW Baltimore Avenue (313 m)
   - More residential neighborhoods

7. **Arrive at St. Johns** (45.593, -122.747)
   - Reward yourself at the bridge end!
   - Great cafe nearby

### Key Highlights
- 🌉 St. Johns Bridge — iconic Portland landmark, gorgeous from above
- 📈 Challenging climb — 800+ ft gain
- 🌲 Forest Park exit — start in the lush forest
- 📸 Bridge views — top of St. Johns Bridge has 360° views
- 🏘️ Neighborhood ride — peaceful corridors

### Preparation Tips
- Bring water — the climb is long
- Start with a good breakfast — you'll need the energy
- The bridge is best in calm weather
- Wear bright/reflective clothing
- There's a great cafe in St. Johns at the end
