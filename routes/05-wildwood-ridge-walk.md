# Wildwood Ridge Trail Walk — Forest Park Ridgeline

**Category:** Walking  
**Distance:** ~3.3 miles (one-way) | ~6.6 mi round trip  
**Difficulty:** Moderate-to-Strenuous (steady uphill, ridge exposure)  
**Duration:** ~64 minutes one-way  
**Elevation Gain:** ~500–700 ft  
**Surface:** Natural trail (dirt, roots, rocky sections)

---

## Route Overview

This walk follows the **high ridgeline spine** of Forest Park from the central Forest Park area northward along **Northwest Skyline Boulevard** and beyond. It's an excellent option for runners looking for a *gradual climb with big payoff* — you'll pass through alternating thick forest and open ridge-top clearings with panoramic views of the Portland metro and surrounding Cascade peaks.

This is NOT a flat "utility" walk — it's a cardiovascular workout disguised as a nature hike.

---

## Directions (Two-Leg ridge push)

### Start Point: Balanced Rock area
**Coordinates:** 45.57088, -122.77165 (Forest Park core)

### Leg 1: Skyline Boulevard Ridgeline (2.2 mi)
1. Start at **Balanced Rock junction** — one of Forest Park's most recognizable trail landmarks
2. Turn **north** and begin ascending onto higher ridgeline
3. Cross onto **Northwest Skyline Boulevard** (~0.3 mi up)
4. Transition from dense forest to **oak-grassland savanna** — views expand rapidly
5. Continue north on Skyline Blvd, climbing steadily
6. Open **viewshed** opens up: full daytime downtown Portland skyline visible (on clear days)
7. Southeast view: **Mt. Hood** on clear mornings (best 10 mile window is 8–10 AM)
8. Pass through **Southwest Barnes Road** junction (~1.5 mi mark) — water/snack stop potential
9. Pass through **Southwest Leahy Road** — steepest hump in the walk
10. Continue reaching the **highest ridgeline point** (~45.581, -122.790) at ~2.2 mi

### Leg 2: Optional Forest Extension (1.1 mi)
11. From the ridgeline high point, turn onto **Northwest Germantown Road** heading south
12. Descent through mixed forest (~0.7 mi) with creek drainages on either side
13. Pick up **Northwest College Drive** for final descent
14. End at the combo Wildwood/Skyline junction (~45.581, -122.790 turnaround)

**Total (one-way):** ~3.3 mi  
**Round-trip cumulative:** ~6.6 mi  
**Elevation at peak:** ~800–1,100 ft

---

## Seasonal Notes

- 🌸 **Spring (May–June):** Ferns miniature green waves every few feet
- 🦌 **Summer:** Wildflowers on the ridgeline open meadows (camas lily prairie field)
- 🍂 **Fall:** Colors warming up in oak lane maple opal glow in canopy gaps
- ❄️ **Winter:** Ridge is exposed and can be windy — watch for ice on the North side
- 🚨 **Fire Season (Jul–Sep):** Clear but smoky; best views often close to the valley below

---

## Safety

- ⚠️ Ridge walking has **wind exposure** — gusts can catch you off-guard
- ⚠️ Narrow shoulders on Skyline Blvd sections — **stay RIGHT**
- ⚠️ This is active cycling country — **yell before stepping into the road**
- ⚠️ No cell signal at the ridgeline high point — tell someone your plan
- ☠️ Poison oak present in the forest understory layers — stay on established trail
