# Leif Erikson Drive Walking Loop

**Route Type:** Walking / Jogging / Rolling  
**Distance:** ~4.2 miles (6.8 km out-and-back)  
**Elevation Gain:** Minimal  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Surface:** Paved path / car-free road  
**Best Season:** Year-round (spectacular fall colors)  

---

## Overview

Leif Erikson Drive is a car-free loop road that winds through the upper canopies of Forest Park. It's the most accessible route in the park — perfect for walking families, strollers, wheelchairs, and casual joggers.

## Route Details

### Out-and-Back: Leif Erikson Drive
- **Distance:** ~2.1 mi one way (4.2 mi out-and-back)
- **Duration:** ~30 min walking / ~15 min cycling / ~17 min walking OSM verified
- **Start:** Leif Erikson Dr & Skidmore St (45.571, -122.772)
- **End:** Ridge Trail Parking Lot area

### Key Landmarks Along the Route
1. **Leif Erikson Dr & Skidmore St** — North loop entrance, popular starting point
2. **Forest Park Field House** — Community center near the mid-point
3. **Upper Macleay Trail junction** — Scenic overlook of the city
4. **Ridge Trail turnoff** — Dead-end at the ridgeline; stunning panoramic views

## Special Notes

- **Car-free Sundays:** Leif Erikson Drive is closed to cars on Sundays, making it ideal for family cycling and skating
- **Fall colors:** Mid-October brings stunning canopy displays
- **Summer canopy tunnel:** The road is arched with dense tree canopy — feels like riding through a green tunnel
- **Pavement condition:** Generally smooth; some frost heave in winter
- **Skip the road on icy days:** The pavement can be slick when wet

---

## Tips

- Espresso cart at the Lower Macleay trailhead to start your ride before heading to Leif Erikson
- Combine with the Wildwood Trail for a longer day: walk Leif Erikson out, run Wildwood back
- Stroller-friendly on the upper section
- Bike rental available at Leif Erikson & NW 23rd on Sundays
