# Lower Macleay → Wildwood Trail Junction Walking Route

**Type:** Walking/Hiking
**OSM Verified Distance:** 7,932 m one way
**Estimated Duration:** ~95 min walking (5 km/h)
**Elevation Gain:** ~750 ft
**Difficulty:** Moderate-Challenging

---

## Route Overview

A longer walk from the Lower Macleay trailhead deep into the heart of Forest Park, arriving at the Wildwood Trail junction near NW Cornell Road. This route takes you through the eastern section of the park, crossing several creeks and ascending through old-growth forest.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions (OSM Verified)

1. **Start:** Lower Macleay Park trailhead (45.534, -122.713)
2. Head northwest on **Lower Macleay Trail** (~392 m)
3. Turn left onto **NW Thurman Street** (~853 m) — residential, well-paved
4. Turn right onto **NW 25th Avenue** (~392 m)
5. Turn right onto **NW Lovejoy Street** (~138 m)
6. Continue onto **NW Cornell Road** (~3,787 m) — long, winding stretch through the park
7. Continue onto **NW Thompson Road** (~1,580 m)
8. Turn right onto **NW 53rd Drive** (~822 m)
9. **Arrive:** Wildwood Trail junction (45.543, -122.738)

## Notes

- This is one of the main trans-park routes; connects south and north Forest Park
- NW Cornell Road section is the longest street-based segment; watch for traffic
- Wildwood Trail continues north from this junction for many more miles
- Partway along NW Thompson Road you'll enter the deep forest portion of the park
- Creek crossings near the autumn/winter can be tricky — waterproof footwear recommended
- The route gains significant elevation; it's a leg-burner on the return
