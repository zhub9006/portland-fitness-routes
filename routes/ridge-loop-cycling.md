## Route Description

A scenic circuit through the Germantown Ridge area, connecting Forest Park's western boundary roads. This loop combines moderate climbs with quiet residential streets and panoramic city views. It's ideal for building climbing legs and testing bike handling on varied terrain.

### Clockwise Direction (Recommended)

1. **Start:** NW Wildwood Trail / St. Helens Rd intersection (45.570, -122.769) — main park entrance
2. Head west on **NW Saint Helens Road** (main park arterial)
3. Turn north onto **NW Germantown Road** — the signature climb
4. Ride the ridge with intermittent city views through the canopy
5. At the northern end, turn east onto **NW Kaiser Road**
6. Kaiser Road descends gently toward the south
7. Turn south onto **NW Laidlaw Road** — a quiet, flat residential connector
8. Follow Laidlaw back to the Wildwood Trail / St. Helens Rd area
9. Complete the loop

### Reverse Direction (Downhill Focus)

1. Start at Wildwood Trail / St. Helens Rd
2. Head south on NW Laidlaw Rd (flat)
3. Climb NW Kaiser Road (gentle grade)
4. Descend NW Germantown Road (steep, fast, rewarding)
5. Return to start via NW Saint Helens Rd

## Road Surfaces & Conditions

- All roads are paved asphalt
- Germantown Road is the busiest segment; ride defensively on weekends
- Kaiser Road and Laidlaw Road are very quiet — ideal for focused riding
- Road shoulders vary; Germantown has a narrower shoulder in some sections

## Training Applications

| Type | How to Use |
|------|------------|

| **Tempo Ride** | Steady moderate pace on the full loop; target 75-80% max HR |
| **Interval Training** | Sprint on Kaiser, recover on Laidlaw; repeat 5x |
| **Skills Practice** | Cornering on Germantown curves; descend with confidence |
