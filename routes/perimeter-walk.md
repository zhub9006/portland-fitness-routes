# Forest Park Perimeter Walking Route 🌲🚶

**Route Type:** Walking | **Difficulty:** Moderate | **Elevation:** ~1,000 ft total

---

## Route Overview

Walk the full perimeter of Forest Park, combining paved city streets with park access roads. This route gives you a complete loop around the entire park with ever-changing scenery — from the dense old-growth canopy to open ridgeline views, down to the river flats and back.

## Highlights

- 🌲 **Cornell Road canopy** — One of Portland's most beautiful urban tree tunnels
- 🏔️ **Skyline ridgeline** — Expansive views of Mt. Hood and the Gorge
- 🌉 **St. Johns Bridge** — Iconic suspension bridge as your entry landmark
- 🌊 **Yeon Avenue descent** — Sweeping, fast descent by the river
- 🏡 **NW residential** — Craftsman bungalows and historic homes throughout
- 🍂 **Seasonal shifts** — Spring wildflowers, summer canopy shade, fall foliage, winter rainbows
- 🦅 **Wildlife** — Owls, hawks, deer, and pileated woodpeckers along the whole route

## How to Use This Route

### Walk 1: Southeast Quadrant (1.5 hrs)
1. Start at **Forest Park** (45.536, -122.738)
2. Walk the perimeter south and east to **Channel Avenue**
3. Turn around and retrace OR grab coffee downtown before heading back

### Walk 2: Full Perimeter Loop (3.5 hrs)
1. Start at **Lower Macleay Park** (45.533, -122.713)
2. Follow the full clockwise perimeter as detailed above
3. Finish back at **Lower Macleay** after crossing through downtown
4. **Tip:** Break this into 2 groups — fast walkers take the perimeter loop, walkers meet at downtown

### Walk 3: Half Perimeter (Out-and-Back)
1. Head west on **Cornell Road** → 3,050 m
2. Continue to **Skyline Boulevard** → 3,763 m
3. Turn around at any point and retrace
4. **Recommended turnaround:** Ridge intersection or Germantown junction

## Route Notes

- ⚠️ **Long distance** — This is a big route; bring water and snacks
- ⚠️ **Traffic on some segments** — Stadium Freeway, Going Street, and Commerce can be busy; use crosswalks
- 🌧️ **Rain** — NW Portland gets significant rainfall; waterproof footwear recommended
- 🐕 **Dogs** — Leash laws apply throughout; most trail sections are dog-friendly
- 🚲 **Bikes allowed** — Pedestrians have right-of-way; yield to bikes on shared roads
