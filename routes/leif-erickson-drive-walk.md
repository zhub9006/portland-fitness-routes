# Leif Erikson Drive Out-and-Back Walk

## Route Overview
- **Distance:** ~5 miles (10 miles round trip)
- **Elevation Gain:** ~400 ft
- **Terrain:** Paved road, well-maintained
- **Difficulty:** 🟢 Easy
- **Start:** Lower Macleay Park entrance (NW Cornell Rd & NW 29th Ave)
- **GPS Coordinates:** 45.5709, -122.7716

## Description
Leif Erikson Drive is the main car-free road running through Forest Park. It's the most popular route for walkers and runners. This out-and-back covers the scenic lower section from Lower Macleay Park to the Hoyt Arboretum area.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions
1. Start at Lower Macleay Park entrance (NW Cornell Rd & NW 29th Ave)
2. Head northeast on Leif Erikson Drive (paved, car-free)
3. Pass the Wildwood Trail intersection at ~1 mile
4. Continue through the forested corridor alongside Balch Creek
5. Arrive at Hoyt Arboretum area at ~2.5 miles (turnaround point)
6. Retrace your steps back to the start

## Key Points
- Wildwood Trail intersection
- Hoyt Arboretum
- Pittock Mansion view (on clear days)

## Best For
- Beginners, casual walkers, dog walkers
- Family outings
- Recovery walks

## Notes
- Road is closed to cars on weekdays (dawn to ~10am) and all day Sundays
- Bring water — no fountains on the trail
- Dogs must be leashed
