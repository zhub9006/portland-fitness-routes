# Leif Erikson Drive Loop — Cycling Route

**Category:** Cycling  
**Distance:** ~4.2 miles (point-to-point; double for full loop)  
**Difficulty:** Easy (car-free path, suitable for all skill levels)  
**Duration:** ~35 minutes one-way  
**Surface:** Paved multi-use path  
**Best For:** Families, beginners, recreational riders

---

## Route Description

Leif Erikson Drive is the premier **car-free cycling route** in Forest Park. The road is temporarily closed to vehicular traffic on select days and hours, opening up a safe, scenic ride through an old-growth forest canopy. The road follows the lower portion of the park, winding along creeks and through "tunnel-like" sections where century-old fir and cedar create a natural arch overhead.

> **⚠️ Before you go:** Verify the current road closure schedule. Leif Erikson Drive typically closes to cars on Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday all day, and Saturday afternoons. [Check Portland.gov for updates](https://www.portland.gov/transportation/bike-leif-erickson-drive-closures).

---

## Driving Directions (Route)

**Start:** Leif Erikson & Northwest Saint Helens junction (45.549, -122.734)  
**End:** Near the north end at the viewpoint area (45.558, -122.757)

1. Begin at the lower pull-out parking area off Saint Helens Road
2. Ride north on **Northwest Leif Erikson Drive** — wide paved path
3. Pass through the first "tree tunnel" — dense canopy overhead (~0.5 mi in)
4. Cross **Henderson Creek** — listen for songbirds
5. Junction with Wildwood Trail (unpaved foot-only crossing at ~1.0 mi)
6. Continue north through wet-fern corridor
7. Pass the **MacLeay Trail spur** on the left (~1.5 mi)
8. Gradual climb continues toward the ridge junction with Saltzman Road (~2.1 mi)
9. **Turnaround point:** Near the junction with **Northwest Saltzman Road** (~2 mi from start)
10. Return via the same route

**Total round trip:** ~4.2 miles  
**Estimated time:** 30–40 minutes (inclusive of turnaround)

## Safety & Rules

- 🚲 Ride single-file in the tree tunnels (path is 10–12 ft wide — tight for two bikes)
- ⚡ E-bikes NOT permitted in the closed-to-cars hours
- 🔇 Sound off before passing hikers — use a bell or call out
- 🐕 Dogs must be leashed (6 ft max)
- ⬅️ Stay RIGHT to share with runners and walkers
- 🚧 Obey all vehicle-open/off signage — do not ride when vehicles are allowed

## Highlights

- 🌳 Tree-canopy tunnel effect (spectacular in morning mist)
- 🦆 Creek-side duck and heron sightings at Henderson Creek
- 🍃 Year-round fern understory
- 🌲 Historic planted redwoods near the southern end (planted 1930s)
- 🦌 Seasonal deer and raccoon family groups
