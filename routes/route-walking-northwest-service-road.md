# Route: Northwest Service Road Walk (Easy) 🟢

## Overview
| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Walking |
| **Difficulty** | Easy |
| **Distance** | ~2 miles (St. Johns Bridge NW Service Road to Faraday Rd) |
| **Elevation Gain** | Minimal |
| **Terrain** | Wide gravel/dirt path, shaded canopy |
| **Surface** | Crushed gravel, packed dirt |
| **Start** | St. Johns Bridge NW Service Road parking area |
| **End** | Faraday Road area |

## Description
A peaceful walk along the Northwest Service Road through the heart of Forest Park. This wide, flat path runs parallel to the main roads above and offers excellent birdwatching opportunities under the old-growth canopy. It's ideal for recovery walks, nature observation, and family strolls — the minimal elevation gain makes it accessible to all fitness levels.

## Route Description
1. Park at the St. Johns Bridge NW Service Road parking area (NW St. Andrews St & NW Skyline Blvd)
2. Enter the Northwest Service Road — a wide gravel path running north-south
3. Walk north through dense forest — listen for woodpeckers and warblers
4. Pass under the St. Johns Bridge overhead — a striking engineering landmark
5. Continue north toward Faraday Road — the path gradually curves
6. Turn around at your preferred point and return

## Key Points
- **St. Johns Bridge:** Historic 1931 suspension bridge with Gothic towers — scenic photo opportunity from below
- **Canopy Walk:** Old-growth Douglas fir and western red cedar form a lush ceiling
- **Bird Habitat:** Rich bird life due to proximity to the Willamette River and forest canopy
- **Faraday Road:** Northern endpoint with road access for pickup/dropoff
- **River Views:** Occasional glimpses of the Willamette River through the trees

## Best For
- Recovery walks after harder workouts
- Nature observation and birdwatching
- Family strolls with kids or dogs
- Cool evening walks (shaded and flat)

## Trail Conditions & Preparedness
| Condition | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Dry | Comfortable walking shoes; gravel may be loose |
| Wet | Path can be slippery on gravel sections — use caution |
| Winter | Minimal ice risk due to low elevation and tree cover |

### What to Bring
- **Water:** No fountains on the service road — bring your own
- **Binoculars:** Excellent for birdwatching
- **Camera:** St. Johns Bridge views and forest scenery
- **Layers:** Shaded path stays cool even in summer

## Seasonal Tips
- **Spring:** Beautiful understory blooms; warblers migration season
- **Summer:** Dense canopy → cool and shaded; mosquitoes possible near the river
- **Fall:** Salmon migration in the Willamette River nearby; foliage colors
- **Winter:** Quiet and peaceful; occasional rain

## Nearby Amenities
- **St. Johns Bridge Parking:** Free street parking at NW Skyline Blvd & NW St. Andrews St
- **Cathedral Park:** Just across the St. Johns Bridge — great extension if driving
- **NW 26th Ave:** Restaurants and coffee shops nearby for post-walk refreshments
