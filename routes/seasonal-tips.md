# Seasonal Training Tips

Tips for training in Forest Park throughout the year.

---

## Spring (March – May)

- **Trail conditions:** Wet, muddy, and slightly slippery
- **Best time:** Mid-morning to early afternoon after dew dries
- **Gear:** Waterproof shoes/boots, gaiters for deep mud
- **Training focus:** Base building, mileage accumulation
- **Watch for:** Trail erosion — avoid muddy sections that are closing in

## Summer (June – August)

- **Trail conditions:** Dry north-facing; south-facing slopes can be hard-packed and dusty
- **Best time:** Early morning (before 10 AM) — heat and UV exposure
- **Gear:** Sun protection, breathable layers, plenty of water (carry at least 1L)
- **Training focus:** Speed work, intervals, hill repeats
- **Watch for:** Ticks in grassy/leafy areas; dehydration risk

## Fall (September – November)

- **Trail conditions:** Cool, crisp, generally the best season
- **Best time:** Any time — comfortable temps and beautiful colors
- **Gear:** Light layers, rain jacket (fall storms arrive late October)
- **Training focus:** Long rides/runs, tempo work, strength training on stair routes
- **Watch for:** Falling leaves covering roots and rocks; earlier sunset

## Winter (December – February)

- **Trail conditions:** Rainy, icy at higher elevations
- **Best time:** Midday when temperatures are highest
- **Gear:** Waterproof everything, studded bike tires, reflective gear, headlamp
- **Training focus:** Consistency, base mileage, indoor cross-training
- **Watch for:** Ice on Leif Erikson Drive above 500 ft; reduced visibility

---

## Pro Tips

- **Consistency beats intensity** — show up regularly, even if it's just 30 minutes
- **Layer up** — Portland weather changes fast, plan for 3 seasons in one ride
- **Backup plan** — keep an indoor alternative for extreme weather days
- **Community rides** — check the club calendar for organized group outings

---

*Stay prepared, stay safe, and keep training!*
