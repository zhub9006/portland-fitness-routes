# Skyline Boulevard Walking Route 🏔️

## Overview

The Skyline Boulevard walk is an iconic Forest Park experience — a long ridgeline route wrapping around the park with sweeping panoramic views of Mount Hood, the Coast Range, and the Vancouver, WA skyline.

- **OSM Verified:** ~11,622 m full loop
- **Distance:** ~11.6 km (~7.2 mi)
- **Elevation:** ~800 ft cumulative
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (length and elevation)
- **Surface:** Paved roads + some dirt trail segments
- **Estimated Walking Time:** ~3 hours at a moderate pace

---

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

### Start: NW Skyline Blvd at Cornell Rd

1. **Start:** NW Skyline Blvd & NW Cornell Rd
   - **Coordinates:** 45.533, -122.756
2. Head west on NW Skyline Blvd
3. Follow Skyline Blvd as it curves north through the forest
4. Cross NW Germantown Rd intersection
5. Continue north on Skyline Blvd through the ridgeline
6. Pass NW Skyliners Dr — a connector to the Leif Erikson loop
7. Continue west on Skyline Blvd toward the Linnton area
8. Descend slightly toward NW Frontier Way

### Western Segment

9. Turn right onto NW Frontier Way (a quiet residential connector)
10. Follow Frontier Way back toward the park's western edge
11. Continue north on the creek side toward NW Youngs Rd

### Return via NW Cornell Rd

12. Turn right onto NW Cornell Rd (heading south)
13. Follow NW Cornell Rd for ~1 km through forest
14. Arrive at NW Skyline Blvd & Cornell Rd — completing the loop

## What to Look For

- **Mt. Hood views** on clear days from the ridgeline
- **Pileated woodpeckers** — listen for their distinctive drumming
- **Bigleaf maples** — some of the largest in the city
- **Seasonal wildflowers** — California buttercup in spring, aster in fall

---

## Safety Notes

- Many segments have no sidewalk — walk facing traffic
- Visibility can be low on the ridgetop in fog
- Carry water — no fountains on this route
- The route crosses several quiet roads — always watch for cars at intersections
