# Forest Park Skyline Cycling Route 🌟

**Category:** Cycling  
**Distance:** ~12.2 miles (lollipop-style loop)  
**Difficulty:** Moderate – skilled bike handling on narrow ridge roads  
**Duration:** ~19–21 minutes at pace per lap; plan 1–2 hrs with stops  
**Elevation Gain:** ~1,800 ft total  
**Surface:** Paved arterials (some narrow, some scenic ridges)

---

## Route Overview

This is the **premium cycling route** in Forest Park — a lollipop-style loop that connects low-elevation Leif Erikson Drive with high-elevation Skyline Boulevard. You'll spend the best part of the ride on pothole-free **Northwest Skyline Boulevard**, a scenic 6-mile spine through the heart of the park with panoramic views of the Willamette Valley and Mt. Hood on clear days.

---

## Directions (Clockwise Loop)

### Leg A: Lower Access (2.0 mi)
1. Start at Forest Park visitor area / Balanced Rock (45.570, -122.772)
2. Ride **north** on forest roads toward NW Saint Helens Road
3. Turn west on **Northwest 53rd Drive** (0.8 mi) — newer residential section
4. Turn north on **Northwest Thompson Road** (1.2 mi) — quiet, tree-lined
5. Arrive at **Northwest Skyline Boulevard** (~4.0 mi from start)

### Leg B: The Skyline Spine 🏔️ (6.0 mi) — SAMAT
6. Turn **right (north)** onto **Northwest Skyline Boulevard**
7. This is the best 6 miles of cycling in Portland — continuous ridgeline views
8. Pass viewpoints overlooking **Washington Park**, **Oregon Zoo**, city core
9. As you ride north, the forest transitions from dense to oak-grassland canopy
10. Enjoy **sweeping views** of Mt. Hood, Mt. St. Helens, and the Coast Range on clear days
11. Watch for **wildflowers** (camas, Oregon grape) May–June
12. Bike bottleneck: this section has narrow shoulders — **ride defensively**

### Leg C: Gravitational Return (5.2 mi)
13. At the northern end of Skyline Blvd (~10 mi), turn **right (east)** onto **Northwest Germantown Road**
14. Fast descent (~1 mile) — checker your brakes!
15. Cross over to **West Burnside Road** at the bottom of Germantown Hill
16. Near terminus, pick up **Southwest Barnes Road** → **Southwest Leahy Road** → **Southwest Taylor Street**
17. Return to the southern access loop to complete the circuit

**✨ Total:** ~12.2 miles  
**⏱️ Estimated time:** 1.5–2.5 hrs (with viewpoint stops and photo ops)

## Equipment

- 🛞 Road or gravel bike (tires 28mm+ for vibration damping on Skyline)
- 🔴 Front & rear lights (ridgeline visibility changes fast)
- 💧 1–2 bottles (no water fountains on route)
- 🗺️ Offline GPS/map (cell signal fades on Skyline)
- 🧵 Gore-Tex layer (ridgeline wind can be 15°F colder than the Valley below)

---

## Safety Notes

- ⚠️ **Skyline Blvd** is scenic but narrow with **45 mph car traffic** on adjacent sections — ride the shoulder confidently
- ⚠️ Several blind corners along Skyline Blvd — stay right
- ⚠️ **Forest fire season** (July–Oct) — check trail closures
- ⚠️ **Morning fog** can linger in the valleys while it's clear on top — descend with caution visibility drops
- 🐦 **Year-round** raptor activity on the ridgelines — watch for circling hawks

## Highlights

- 🥞 Best 6 miles of cycling in Portland (locals even call it the "Skyline Spin")
- 🌄 Annual lake view in Clear if the weather cooperates
- 🏃 Pursuit on ridgeline perfect for drafting and training
- 🦌 Oak savanna grasslands where you can see prairies year-round
- 🌲 Mix of urban-influencing views with total-forest solitude
