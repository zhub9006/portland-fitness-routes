# Leif Erikson Drive Walking Route

## Overview
- **Distance:** ~4.2 miles (car-free loop)
- **Difficulty:** Easy (great for beginners and families)
- **Duration:** ~35 minutes at a relaxed pace
- **Surface:** Paved path (Leif Erikson Drive)
- **Elevation Gain:** Minimal

## Route Description
A beautiful car-free loop through old-growth forest on Leif Erikson Drive. Access via Northwest Saint Helens Road at the trailhead parking area. The route follows a one-way path through towering tree canopies with fern overhead and creekside riding/walking opportunities.

## Key Waypoints
| Point | Coordinates | Description |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Start/End | 45.549, -122.734 | Leif Erikson & Saint Helens junction |
| Mid-point | 45.558, -122.725 | Mid-forest canopy tunnel section |

## Highlights
- Tunnels of old-growth trees (canopy overhead)
- Fern-lined creek beds
- Car-free environment — safe for walking and families
- Wildlife spotting opportunities (deer, coyotes, banana slugs)
- Historic Upper Macleay Park proximity

## Practical Notes
- **Note:** Leif Erikson Drive is closed to vehicles certain hours/days — verify before walking!
  - typically Mon/Tue/Thu all day and Saturday afternoons
- Best in dry weather; muddy conditions Nov–May
- Start near Upper Macleay Park for a pleasant forest immersion
