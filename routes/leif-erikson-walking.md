# Leif Erikson Drive Walking Route

## Overview
- **Type:** Walking / Jogging
- **Distance:** ~4.2 miles (6.8 km) out-and-back
- **Elevation Gain:** ~400 ft (120 m)
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Surface:** Paved road (car-free on Sundays)

## Route Description
Leif Erikson Drive is the scenic perimeter road that loops around the northern section of Forest Park. The road is car-free on Sundays and offers a shaded canopy tunnel in summer and stunning fall colors. This out-and-back route follows the road from the southern entrance to the northern end and back.

## Detailed Waypoints

### Southern Start: NW Saint Helens Road & Leif Erikson Drive (45.549, -122.734)
- Park near the Forest Park entrance at the northwest end of NW Saint Helens Road.

### Outbound: Leif Erikson Drive Northbound
- **Distance:** ~3.4 km (2.1 miles) one way
- **Duration:** ~25-30 min walking
- **Key Points:**
  - NW Saint Helens Road (45.549, -122.734) — starting point
  - Via Saltzman Road area (45.561, -122.750) — midway point
  - Leif Erikson Drive continues through Forest Park canopy
  - NW Germantown Road connector (45.570, -122.769) — Wildwood Trail access

### Turnaround: Northern End
- **Location:** Near Leif Erikson Dr & Skidmore St (45.571, -122.772)
- **Notes:** Great viewpoint; the road ends near this intersection.

### Return: Same route southbound
- **Duration:** ~25-30 min walking
- **Total round trip:** ~6.8 km / ~60-70 min

## Key Landmarks
- **Leif Erikson Drive:** Car-free on Sundays — perfect for walking
- **Forest Park Field House:** Community center near the route
- **NW Germantown Road:** Connector to Wildwood Trail

## Tips
- Best on Sunday mornings when the road is closed to cars.
- Shade canopy is thickest in June-July — bring a flashlight for the darkest stretch.
- Fall foliage is spectacular along this stretch (late October).
- Watch for cyclists and joggers on weekends.
