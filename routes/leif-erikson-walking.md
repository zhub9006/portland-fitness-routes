# Leif Erikson Drive Walking & Jogging Route

## Overview

- **Type:** Walking / Jogging
- **OSM Verified Distance:** 2,129 m one-way; 10,403 m (full loop)
- **Duration:** ~5 min one-way; ~17 min walking full loop
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Elevation Gain:** ~400 ft
- **Surface:** Paved road

## Start Point

- **Coordinates:** 45.549, -122.734
- **Location:** NW Saint Helens Rd & Leif Erikson Dr (south end of the drive)

## Route Description

Leif Erikson Drive is a **car-free loop road** (closed to cars on Sundays) that winds through the heart of Forest Park. The drive is approximately 10.4 km (6.5 miles) long and is shaded by a dense canopy of Douglas firs in summer, with stunning fall colors in autumn. It's the premier walking and jogging route in Portland.

### One-Way Out-and-Back (Lower Macleay → Leif Erikson Dr & Skidmore St)

```
Start: NW Saint Helens Rd & Leif Erikson Dr (45.549, -122.734)
→ North along Leif Erikson Drive / NW Saint Helens Rd
→ Turnoff at Leif Erikson Dr & Skidmore St (45.571, -122.772)
→ Out-and-back return
Distance: 2,129 m one-way (~4.2 mi total)
```

### Full Loop (via NW Saint Helens Rd → Germantown Rd → Skyline Blvd)

```
Start: NW Saint Helens Rd & Leif Erikson Dr (45.549, -122.734)
→ North: NW Saint Helens Rd (3,764 m segment from Bridge Ave)
→ NW Germantown Rd (3,356 m)
→ NW Skyline Boulevard (4,529 m)
→ NW Thunder Crest Drive (283 m)
→ Return start
Total: 10,403 m (~6.5 mi)
```

## Highlights

- 🌲 **Canopy Tunnel:** Dense Douglas fir canopy creates a natural tunnel effect in summer
- 🍂 **Fall Foliage:** One of the best spots in Portland for autumn leaf viewing
- 🚗 **Car-Free Sundays:** Street is closed to vehicular traffic on Sundays — perfect for families
- 🦌 **Wildlife:** Deer, raccoons, and varied bird life along the route
- 📸 **Photo Ops:** Multiple trailside viewpoints and old-growth groves

## Running Club Notes

- **Best for:** Easy recovery runs, long walks, family outings
- **Ideal time:** Early morning on Sundays (car-free) or Saturday mornings
- **Bring:** Layers (it can be 10-15°F cooler in the tree canopy)
- **Etiquette:** Ring a bell or announce when passing walkers on the narrow road
