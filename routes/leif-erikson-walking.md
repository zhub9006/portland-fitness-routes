# Leif Erikson Drive Walking Route 🚶

Leif Erikson Drive is a scenic, car-free (on Sundays) loop road that circles the interior of Forest Park. It's the most accessible walking route in the park — paved, wide, and shaded by a tunnel of Doug fir and western red cedar canopy.

### Waypoints
1. **Start:** Leif Erikson Dr & Skidmore St — 45.571, -122.772
2. **Mid:** Tree canopy tunnel (summer shade)
3. **Key Landmarks:** Wildflower meadows, bird-watching spots
4. **End:** Return to Skidmore St via full loop

### Description
Start at the Leif Erikson Drive & Skidmore St intersection in the northwest corner of the park. The road winds gently through dense forest, with a remarkable canopy tunnel in summer when the Douglas firs form a green arch overhead. In autumn, the route explodes with fall colors from the maples and alders lining the road.

On Sundays, the road is closed to motor vehicles, making it the ideal location for walking, jogging, or roller skating. Even on weekdays, traffic is very light (residents only) — still pleasant for walking.

### Highlights
- **Canopy Tunnel:** Dense Doug fir canopy in summer — feels like walking through a green cathedral
- **Fall Colors:** Peak foliage typically mid-October through early November
- **Wildlife:** Frequent sightings of deer, owls, and woodpeckers
- **Car-Free Sundays:** Portland Bureau of Transportation closes the road weekly

### Notes
- Paved surface — no need for trail shoes
- Gentle Grades — suitable for all fitness levels
- Best time: early morning on weekdays or Sunday mornings for the quietest experience
