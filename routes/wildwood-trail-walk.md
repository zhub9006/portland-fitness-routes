# Wildwood Trail Walk 🥾

**Route Type:** Walking  
**Distance:** 3,379 m (Macleay → Witch's Castle); 4,594 m (Macleay → Pittock)  
**Elevation Gain:** ~500 ft (Macleay → Witch's Castle); ~700 ft (Macleay → Pittock)  
**Estimated Duration:** 7–11 min walk (one-way)

## Overview

The Wildwood Trail is the crown jewel of Portland's urban hiking. This ancient trail winds through old-growth Douglas fir and western red cedar forest for over 7 miles, passing through the heart of Forest Park.

## Route 1: Lower Macleay → Witch's Castle

- **Start:** Lower Macleay Trail trailhead (45.5344, -122.7132)
- **End:** Witch's Castle (Stone House ruins) (45.528, -122.725)
- **Distance:** 3,379 m
- **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate

### Turn-by-Turn Directions
1. Start at the Lower Macleay Trail parking area off NW 31st Avenue
2. Follow the Lower Macleay Trail north through the forest
3. The trail rises through old-growth forest with seasonal creek crossings
4. Continue on Wildwood Trail heading northeast
5. Arrive at the Witch's Castle (Stone House ruins) — a historic site, not for climbing

### Highlights
- Old-growth Douglas fir and western red cedar
- Seasonal creek crossings
- Moderate elevation climb
- Moss-covered forest floor

## Route 2: Lower Macleay → Pittock Mansion

- **Start:** Lower Macleay Trail trailhead (45.5344, -122.7132)
- **End:** Pittock Mansion (45.5252, -122.7162)
- **Distance:** 4,594 m
- **Difficulty:** Moderate

### Turn-by-Turn Directions
1. Start at the Lower Macleay Trail parking area
2. Follow Wildwood Trail northeast through the forest
3. Trail passes through dense old-growth with creek crossings
4. Emerge near NW Pittock Drive
5. Follow Pittock Drive and Pittock Avenue to the mansion

### Highlights
- All the benefits of the Witch's Castle route
- Continue to the iconic Pittock Mansion with panoramic views
- Historic mansion with tours available
- Views of the Willamette Valley and Mt. Hood on clear days
