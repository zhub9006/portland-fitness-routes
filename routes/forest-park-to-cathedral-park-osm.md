# Route: Forest Park to Cathedral Park (OSM Verified)

- **Type:** Cycling / Running
- **Distance:** ~3.2 km (2.0 miles)
- **Duration:** ~12 min cycling / ~25 min running
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Start:** Forest Park interior (45.536, -122.739)
- **End:** Cathedral Park / St. Johns Bridge area (45.523, -122.701)
- **Source:** OpenStreetMap routing analysis

## Description
A fast route from Forest Park down through NW 25th Avenue and over to the St. Johns Bridge area. The route combines Forest Park trail access with NW 25th Avenue's bike-friendly corridor and ends at the iconic Cathedral Park under the St. Johns Bridge — a perfect finish line for group runs or rides.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions (from OSM)
1. **Start** at Forest Park interior entry (NW Engleman St area, 45.536, -122.739)
2. Head west on **NW Cornell Road** — 3,741 m
3. Turn south on **NW Lovejoy Street** — 138 m
4. Continue on **NW 25th Avenue** — 75 m
5. Brief connector segments through residential streets — 150 m
6. Continue on **NW 25th Avenue** — 551 m (bike lane)
7. Turn onto **NW Vaughn Street** — 312 m
8. Continue on **NW Wardway Street** — 443 m
9. Turn onto **NW Nicolai Street** — 219 m
10. Merge onto **NW Saint Helens Road** — 2,446 m heading south
11. Continue on **NW Saint Helens Road** — 3,072 m
12. Arrive at **Cathedral Park / St. Johns Bridge area** (45.523, -122.701)

## Key Features
- NW 25th Avenue: dedicated bike lane for much of the route
- Scenic descent from Forest Park to the waterfront
- Cathedral Park offers a large open area for cool-down stretches
- St. Johns Bridge: Portland's most photographed landmark

## Facilities at Finish
- **Cathedral Park:** Open green space for stretching
- **St. Johns Bridge pedestrian walkway** — access under the bridge
- **Drinking water:** Fountain near St. Johns Bridge entry
- **Parking:** NW 30th Ave & NW Savier St lot (5 min walk from finish)

## Route Notes
- The route has approximately 400 ft of elevation loss from start to finish
- NW 25th Avenue is one-way southbound during morning rush — better for afternoon/evening rides
- Saturday morning: NW 25th Avenue section is low-traffic with bike families
