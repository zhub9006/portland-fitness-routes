# 🚶 Walking Routes Around Forest Park, Portland

## 1. Lower Macleay to Forest Park Heart
- **Distance:** 6.5 miles
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (beginner-friendly with gradual climb)
- **Duration:** ~88 minutes
- **Start Coordinates:** 45.534, -122.713 (Lower Macleay)
- **End Coordinates:** 45.571, -122.772 (Forest Park center)
- **Route:** Lower Macleay Trail → Northwest Leif Erikson Drive (narrow section) → Forest Park core
- **Road Sections:** Northwest 31st Avenue, Northwest Vaughn Street, **Northwest Saint Helens Road** (key arterial through the park), Leif Erikson Drive
- **Highlights:** Historic Upper Macleay Park, beautiful fern-lined creek beds on the Leif Erikson stretch, old-growth forest vibes
- **Route Details:** Trail starts at Lower Macleay parking area, follows the Leif Erikson Drive corridor through dense old-growth canopy before reaching the Forest Park heart at the intersection of NW Saint Helens Road and NW Leif Erikson Drive.

**Waypoints (route geometry):**
- Start: 45.534, -122.713
- Via: NW Leif Erikson Drive corridor (45.549, -122.734)
- Forest Park Heart: 45.571, -122.772
- Total straight-line route through Forest Park interior

## 2. Wildwood Ridge Trail Walk
- **Distance:** 3.3 miles (one-way from Forest Park center to Wildwood Road)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate-to-Strenuous (steady uphill + ridge exposure)
- **Duration:** ~64 minutes
- **Start Coordinates:** 45.571, -122.772 (Forest Park center)
- **End Coordinates:** 45.581, -122.790 (Wildwood Trail area)
- **Route:** Forest Park center → **Northwest Saint Helens Road** (1.7 mi) → **Northwest Bridge Avenue** → **Northwest Germantown Road** (3.4 mi) → **Northwest Skyline Boulevard** → Northwest College Drive
- **Highlights:** Panoramic ridge-line views of the Portland metro, Washington Park, Mt. Hood on clear days, wildflower meadows in spring
- **Road Sections:** NW Saint Helens Road, NW Bridge Avenue, NW Germantown Road, NW Skyline Boulevard

**Route Notes:** This ridge walk offers the best views in Portland. Start early morning for clear views of Mt. Hood. The trail climbs steadily through dense forest before opening to panoramic ridgeline.

## 4. Leif Erikson Canyon Trail Walk
- **Distance:** 2.1 miles (out & back along NW Saint Helens Road corridor)
- **Difficulty:** Easy
- **Duration:** ~45 minutes
- **Start Coordinates:** 45.549, -122.734 (Leif Erikson & Saint Helens junction)
- **End Coordinates:** 45.559, -122.724
- **Route:** Follows the **Northwest Saint Helens Road** tree corridor, then onto **Northwest Kittridge Avenue** and **Northwest Front Avenue**
- **Highlights:** Tunnels of old-growth trees, creekside walking, fern canopy overhead

**Waypoints from route calculation:**
- 45.549, -122.734 (start at Leif Erikson junction)
- Via: NW Saint Helens Road (847m), NW Kittridge Avenue
- End: 45.556, -122.726

## 6. Cathedral Park Walking Route
- **Distance:** ~3.5 miles (ending at the Columbia River)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (downhill to river, uphill return)
- **Duration:** ~90 minutes one way
- **Start Coordinates:** 45.580, -122.762 (Forest Park edge near Cathedral Park)
- **End Coordinates:** 45.591, -122.752 (Cathedral Park at Columbia River)
- **Route:** Forest Park edge → **NW Saint Helens Road** (878m) → **NW Bridge Avenue** → **Saint Johns Bridge** → North Philadelphia Avenue → Cathedral Park
- **Highlights:** Stunning views from Saint Johns Bridge, Columbia River panorama, Cathedral Park beach

**Waypoints from route calculation:**
- Start: 45.580, -122.762
- Via: NW Saint Helens Road, NW Bridge Avenue, Saint Johns Bridge
- End: 45.591, -122.752 (North Leonard Street, Cathedral Park area)

**Route Notes:** This route takes you all the way down to the Willamette River via Cathedral Park. The view from Saint Johns Bridge is spectacular. Best done in the morning to avoid afternoon wind along the river.
