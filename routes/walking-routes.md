## Route W1: Lower Macleay Trailhead → Pittock Mansion Loop

**Difficulty**: 🟡 Moderate  
**Distance**: ~3.0 miles (4,826 m) one way / ~6.1 miles (9,796 m) round trip  
**Estimated Time**: ~63 min one way / ~2 hr 10 min round trip  
**Elevation Gain**: ~900 ft (significant climb to Pittock Mansion)  
**Surface**: Paved roads and some trail sections

### Overview
This is a classic Forest Park area walk that takes you from the beloved Lower Macleay Trailhead up to the historic Pittock Mansion. You'll be rewarded with stunning panoramic views of downtown Portland, Mount Hood, and the Willamette River from the mansion grounds. The return route offers a slightly different path for variety.

- Best done in the morning for clearer views from Pittock Mansion
- The Pittock Mansion grounds are free to explore; the interior requires an admission fee
- Restrooms available at both the trailhead and the mansion
- Great photo opportunity at the mansion viewpoint

## Route W2: Lower Macleay Trailhead → Saint Johns Bridge

**Difficulty**: 🔴 Challenging  
**Distance**: ~4.8 miles (7,686 m) one way  
**Estimated Time**: ~2 hr 40 min one way  
**Elevation Gain**: Moderate with some steep sections along St. Helens Road  
**Surface**: Paved roads and sidewalk

### Overview
A longer walk that follows the base of Forest Park along NW St. Helens Road all the way to the iconic Saint Johns Bridge. This route gives you a great sense of the full north-south extent of Forest Park and culminates at one of Portland's most photographed landmarks — the stunning Gothic-style Saint Johns Bridge spanning the Willamette River.

| 7 | NW Saint Helens Road (south section) | 2,446 m | ~17 min |
| 8 | NW Saint Helens Road (north section) | 3,368 m | ~23 min |
| 9 | NW Bridge Avenue | 854 m | ~6 min |
| 10 | Saint Johns Bridge | 379 m | ~3 min |

**Start**: Lower Macleay Park Trailhead (45.5360° N, 122.7127° W)  
**End**: Saint Johns Bridge (45.5852° N, 122.7646° W)

### 💡 Tips
- This is a long walk — consider arranging a pickup at the bridge or using transit
- The bridge walkway offers spectacular views of the river and forest
- Cathedral Park at the base of the bridge is a great rest stop
- Bring water and snacks; limited services along St. Helens Road
- Best during dry weather; some sections lack shade
