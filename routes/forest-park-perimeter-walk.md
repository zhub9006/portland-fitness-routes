# Forest Park Perimeter Walking Loop

**Route Type:** Walking | **Source:** OpenStreetMap | **Updated:** July 2026

## Overview

The ultimate Forest Park walking challenge — a full perimeter loop around the entire park via roads, residential streets, and trail connections. This route takes you through the diverse neighborhoods and ridgelines surrounding Portland's largest urban forest, covering ~14,600 m of varied terrain with approximately 1,000 m of total elevation gain.

## Route Description

The Forest Park perimeter traces the irregular boundary of the park's 2,000+ hectares of old-growth forest, weaving through residential NW Portland neighborhoods and climbing the Tualatin Mountain ridges. This is not a single trail but a connected series of roads, paths, and trail segments that loop around the park's borders.

### Zones

- **Southern Quadrant** — Lower Macleay to Pittock Mansion (Wildwood Trail) — 3,379 m
- **Eastern Quadrant** — Pittock Mansion to St. Helens Road (ridge roads) — 4,586 m
- **Northern Quadrant** — St. Helens Road via Germantown, Kaiser, Laidlaw (ridge loop) — 4,586 m
- **Western Quadrant** — Ridge Boulevard to Bridge Avenue (ridge roads, residential) — 2,249 m

## Quadrant Breakdown

For a more manageable walk, the loop can be split into four quadrants:

### Southern Quadrant (Easy)
*Lower Macleay → Pittock Mansion via Wildwood Trail*
- Distance: ~3,400 m
- Grade: Moderate climb to Pittock (~500 ft gain)
- Surface: Natural trail, some road connections
- Highlights: Old-growth forest, Witch's Castle, Pittock Mansion

### Eastern Quadrant (Moderate)
*Pittock → Germantown St ridge (east face)*
- Distance: ~4,600 m
- Grade: Rolling, some steep sections
- Surface: Combination of ridge roads and residential streets
- Highlights: Open views of the city and Mt. Hood to the east

### Northern Quadrant (Moderate — Strenuous)
*Germantown → Kaiser → Laidlaw → Ridge Loop*
- Distance: ~4,600 m
- Grade: Significant ridge riding with panoramic views
- Surface: Ridge roads with bike infrastructure
- Highlights: Germantown Ridge panorama, Kaiser Road, Laidlaw

### Western Quadrant (Strenuous)
*Ridge Blvd → Down to Bridge Ave → Crossing St. Johns Bridge → Downtown*
- Distance: ~4,800 m
- Grade: Steep descent to the bridge, then flat along the river
- Surface: Bridge approach, residential descent
- Highlights: St. Johns Bridge views, Willamette River

## Recommended Schedule

- **Full loop**: 2.5–3 hours (weekend start at 8 AM to avoid traffic)
- **Half loop (Southern + Eastern)**: 1–1.25 hours
- **Half loop (Northern + Western)**: 1.25–1.5 hours
- **Best days**: Weekday mornings or Sunday mornings (Leif Erikson/Willamette area are always good)

## Seasonal Considerations

- **Spring (April–June)**: Wildflowers; muddy trail sections
- **Summer (July–August)**: Full canopy cover; bridle paths can be dusty
- **Fall (September–November)**: Peak leaf season; wet leaves on roads
- **Winter (December–March)**: Professional sub-trail sections may have patches of ice; waterproof boots recommended

## Getting There

- **Where to start**: Most start at the Wildwood Trail (Wildwood Trail head) near Skidmore St or the Forest Park Field House lot
- **Parking**: Free street parking on NW Skidmore St, NW King AD, or NW Raleigh St (Lower Macleay)
- **Public Transit**: TriMet bus routes 63 or 281 stop near the park entrance
