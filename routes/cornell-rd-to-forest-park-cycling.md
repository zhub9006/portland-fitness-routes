# Downtown Portland → Forest Park Cycling Route (via NW Cornell Road)

**Type:** Cycling
**OSM Verified Distance:** 6,544 m (Downtown → Forest Park)
**Estimated Duration:** ~19 min cycling (18 km/h)
**Elevation Gain:** ~300 ft (climb on NW Cornell Road)
**Difficulty:** Easy-Moderate (hilly finish)

---

## Route Overview

A fast descent or climb from downtown Portland to Forest Park via NW Cornell Road. This is one of the most popular cycling routes connecting the city center to the park. The route starts flat and residential, then climbs steadily along Cornell Road through the park's eastern boundary.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions (OSM Verified)

1. **Start:** Downtown Portland intersection (45.527, -122.687)
2. Head northwest on **NW 16th Avenue** (~79 m)
3. Turn left onto **NW Flanders Street** (~46 m)
4. Continue briefly on **NW 17th Avenue** (~364 m)
5. Turn right onto **NW Kearney Street** (~951 m)
6. Turn left onto **NW 24th Avenue** (~79 m)
7. Turn left onto **NW Lovejoy Street** (~295 m)
8. Right onto **NW Cornell Road** (~3,741 m) — the big climb
9. **Arrive:** Forest Park center (45.536, -122.739)

## Tips

- NW Cornell Road is the steepest section; shift to a low gear before starting the climb
- Bike lanes are present on most of this route, but be alert on NW Holland Street section
- The descent back to downtown is fast and fun — but watch for stops
- Avoid rush hours (7-9 AM, 4-6:30 PM) if cycling NW Kearney
- Good pre-ride warmup before a Wildwood Trail running session
