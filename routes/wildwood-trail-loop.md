# Wildwood Trail Loop

**Route Type:** Running / Hiking  
**Distance:** ~6.0 miles (10 km)  
**Elevation Gain:** ~550 ft  
**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**Surface:** Dirt trail, packed earth  
**Best Season:** Year-round  

---

## Overview

The Wildwood Trail is the crown jewel of Forest Park, stretching over 30 miles through the old-growth forest. This loop route covers the most popular section from Lower Macleay Trail through the Witch's Castle and back via the lower ridgeline.

## Route Details

### Segment 1: Lower Macleay Trail to Witch's Castle
- **Distance:** ~3.7 km (2.3 mi)
- **Duration:** ~45 min running / ~1 hr hiking
- **Start:** Lower Macleay Trailhead (45.534, -122.713)
- **Key Landmarks:** Old-growth Douglas fir, sword ferns, seasonal creek crossings
- **Notes:** The trail climbs steadily through towering old-growth trees. Watch for partially collapsed seasonal bridges after heavy rain.

### Segment 2: Witch's Castle to Wildwood Trail Main
- **Distance:** ~5.5 km (3.4 mi)
- **Duration:** ~1 hr running / ~1.5 hr hiking
- **Key Landmarks:** Stone House ruins, panoramic city views, moss-covered bridge
- **Notes:** The Witch's Castle (45.528, -122.725) is the most photographed spot in the park. Great rest stop with boulders for sitting.

### Segment 3: Wildwood Trail to Pittock Mansion
- **Distance:** ~4.9 km (3.0 mi)
- **Duration:** ~50 min running / ~1 hr 15 min hiking
- **Start:** Wildwood Trail (45.524, -122.716)
- **End:** Pittock Mansion (45.525, -122.716)
- **Key Landmarks:** Forest Park Field House, Ridge Trail junction, Pittock Mansion
- **Notes:** Paved road section near the end makes for a fast finish. Pittock Mansion has restrooms and water.

### Segment 4: Return via Pittock Drive to Lower Macleay
- **Distance:** ~5.4 km (3.4 mi)
- **Duration:** ~15 min biking / ~45 min hiking
- **Route:** NW Pittock Drive, NW Barnes Road, NW 25th Avenue
- **Notes:** This road segment connects back to the trailhead. Be careful on shared roads.

## Tips

- Bring layers — it can be 10-15°F cooler in the deep forest canopy
- Watch for seasonal creek crossings (especially Nov-Mar)
- The trail is shaded most of the day — perfect for summer running
- Dogs are allowed off-leash on trails (follow local ordinances)
- Parking at Lower Macleay is limited; arrive early on weekends
