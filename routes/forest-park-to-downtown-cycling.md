# Forest Park to Downtown Cycling Route

A thrilling cycling descent from Forest Park through residential NW Portland to downtown and the Willamette River waterfront.

## Route Overview

- **Type:** Cycling
- **Total Distance:** 9,440 m (to downtown); 6,544 m (from downtown)
- **Elevation Gain:** ~500 ft descent (to downtown); ~500 ft climb (return)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (descent) / Challenging (return climb)
- **Surface:** Road; mix of bike lanes, traffic-calmed streets, and park roads

---

## Route Description

This iconic route descends 500+ feet from the forested heights of Forest Park through peaceful NW Portland neighborhoods, emerging at the vibrant downtown and Willamette River waterfront. The descent is exhilarating and the return climb builds serious fitness. A must-do for any Forest Park cycling club.

### Outbound Turn-by-Turn (Forest Park → Downtown)

1. **Start at Forest Park (45.571, -122.772)**
   - Head south through the forest

2. **Take NW Saint Helens Road east** (296 m)
   - Historic road through Forest Park

3. **Continue to NW Bridge Avenue** (854 m)
   - Descend toward the city

4. **Cross the St. Johns Bridge** (1,161 m)
   - Iconic Portland landmark — great views from the bridge!

5. **Continue to NW Philadelphia Avenue** (104 m)
   - Transition to neighborhoods

6. **Head south on NW Willamette Boulevard** (179 m)
   - Scenic river views

7. **Continue on NW Baltimore Avenue** (313 m)
   - Residential streets with good cycling infrastructure

8. **Continue through NW neighborhoods** (various segments)
   - Traffic-calmed residential streets
   - Descend toward the river

9. **Arrive at downtown Portland / Willamette River waterfront** (45.522, -122.672)
   - Celebrate at the waterfront!

### Return Route (Downtown → Forest Park)

The return climb is ~6,544 m with ~500 ft of elevation gain.
- Follow the same route in reverse (or vary with alternate streets)
- The climb back is rewarding and builds serious leg strength
- Take NW Front Avenue for part of the climb
- Re-enter Forest Park via Wildwood Trail / Leif Erikson

### Key Highlights
- 🌉 St. Johns Bridge — iconic crossing with great views
- 🌊 Willamette River waterfront — finish at the water
- 📉 Fast descent — gravity-assisted thrill ride!
- 📈 Return climb — 500 ft gain builds fitness
- 🏘️ Quiet residential neighborhoods — safe cycling

### Preparation Tips
- Bring a water bottle and bail-out options
- Check brakes before the descent!
- The St. Johns Bridge is best in dry conditions
- Wear bright colors — some sections have busy roads
