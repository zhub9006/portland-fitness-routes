# Forest Park to Downtown Portland Cycling Route

## Overview
- **Type:** Cycling
- **Distance:** ~14.6 miles (23.5 km) one way
- **Elevation Gain:** ~900 ft (270 m) descent to downtown
- **Difficulty:** Intermediate
- **Surface:** Paved roads

## Route Description
An exhilarating ride from the forested highways of Forest Park down through NW Portland and into downtown. This route descends from Forest Park through NW Portland, passing through Washington Park and into the downtown core along the Willamette River.

## Detailed Waypoints

### Start: Forest Park Center (45.534, -122.713)
- Begin at Lower Macleay Park / NW 25th Avenue intersection.

### Leg 1: Forest Park Center → NW Saint Helens Road
- **Distance:** ~1.6 km (1.0 miles)
- **Via:** NW 31st Avenue → NW Vaughn Street → NW 25th Avenue
- **OSM Verified:** Via NW Cornell Rd (3,740 m) and NW 25th Ave (789 m)
- **Notes:** Exit the forest area onto the arterial road.

### Leg 2: NW Saint Helens → Yeon Avenue
- **Distance:** ~3.2 km (2.0 miles)
- **Via:** NW Yeon Avenue
- **OSM Verified:** Via NW Saint Helens Road (2,896 m) + NW Bridge Avenue
- **Notes:** NW Yeon is a major north-south arterial; stay alert for traffic.

### Leg 3: Yeon Avenue → Burnside Street
- **Distance:** ~2.4 km (1.5 miles)
- **Via:** NW Burnside Street
- **OSM Verified:** Via Stadium Freeway and NW Going Street
- **Notes:** Burnside is a busy east-west road; consider using the bike lane.

### Leg 4: Burnside → Downtown / Willamette River
- **Distance:** ~3.2 km (2.0 miles)
- **Via:** Naito Parkway (Tunnel through downtown)
- **OSM Verified:** 9,440 m total (~12 min biking)
- **Notes:** Naito Parkway runs along the Willamette River; scenic waterfront finish.

- **Lower Macleay Park:** Forest trail starting point

- **Burnside Bridge:** Iconic Portland bridge (optional extension)
- **Naito Parkway:** Waterfront ride with Willamette River views

## Tips
- The descent from Forest Park is significant — watch your speed on downhill stretches.
- Bring a brake check — the downhill sections are long and steep.
- Best done in the morning before the afternoon traffic builds.
- Water refill stations available at Pioneer Courthouse Square.
