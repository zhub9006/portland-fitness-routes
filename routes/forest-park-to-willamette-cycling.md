# Forest Park to Willamette Valley Cycling Route

## Overview

A dramatic descent ride from West Hills to the Willamette River waterfront – 14.6 miles with a thrilling downhill finish.

## Route Details

- **Distance:** 14.6 miles (23.5 km)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (long downhill out, challenging climb back)
- **Duration:** ~21 min with pace job; plan 1–2 hours round trip
- **Type:** Point-to-point (recommend ride one direction, drive back)
- **Elevation Change:** ~1,200 ft descent, same climb return

## Route Description

Starting from the **Forest Park center area** (45.575, -122.760):

1. **Forest Park center → Northwest Saint Helens Road** (1.5 mi) – Start descending toward the city.
2. **Northwest Saint Helens Road → West Burnside Road** (2.5 mi) – Major descent through the forest.
3. **West Burnside Road → Downtown Portland** (6.5 mi) – Ride the Burnside corridor through the city core.
4. **Optional extension:** Continue to Willamette River waterfront (~1.5 mi extra).

## Highlights

- 🏔️ Dramatic descent through old-growth Forest Park
- 🌆 Bonus time in downtown Portland city core
- 🌊 Reward at Willamette River waterfront with snack stop
- 🚲 Popular route for group rides (slower pace for return climb)

## Best Practices

- ✅ **Ride downhill only; drive back or take it easy**
- ✅ Best combined with lunch downtown
- ✅ Good for group rides – set pace for the climb back
- ⚠️ Return is a significant climb – not recommended for beginners
- ⚠️ Watch for commercial traffic on Burnside

- ⚠️ The descent is fast – control speed on Burnside
- ⚠️ Strong crosswinds possible on exposed ridge
- Cell coverage is good through city sections
- Plan extra time for the climb back if not driving
