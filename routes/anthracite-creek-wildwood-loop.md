# Anthracite Creek → Wildwood Loop 🏃🚴

A multi-segment loop combining the Anthracite Creek trail with the Wildwood Trail for a varied training route through the southern end of Forest Park.

## Route Details

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Distance** | ~8,500 m (~5.3 mi) |
| **Elevation Gain** | ~800 ft |
| **Est. Run Time** | ~35 min (running) |
| **Est. Walk Time** | ~85 min |
| **Surface** | Trail / gravel / road |
| **Difficulty** | Moderate |

### Waypoints
1. **Start:** Lower Macleay Park — 45.534, -122.713
2. **Creek:** Anthracite Creek trail heading east
3. **Ridge:** Trail junction → Wildwood Trail southbound
4. **Key Landmarks:** Creek crossings, fern gardens, stone wall remnants
5. **End:** Return via Macleay to Lower Macleay

### Description
Start at Lower Macleay Park and follow the Anthracite Creek trail eastward through a quieter side valley of Forest Park. The trail follows a small creek through denser understory — more ferns, mosses, and the occasional old stone wall. Connector to the Wildwood Trail, then loop back via the lower Macleay.

This route is quieter than the main Wildwood corridor — ideal for avoiding crowds and experiencing the more secluded southern sections of the park.

### Highlights
- **Creek Scene:** Anthracite Creek provides a soothing audio backdrop
- **Forest Secrets:** Old stone walls, fern gardens, fewer visitors
- **Flexible:** Can be extended by adding Pittock detour (adds ~1.2 mi)
- **Connectivity:** Multiple loop options available

### Notes
- Better suited for trail runners than road cyclists
- Creek crossings can be slippery after rain
- One of the most peaceful routes in Forest Park — bring binoculars for birdwatching
- Connecting to the Round Lake trail adds 2+ km
