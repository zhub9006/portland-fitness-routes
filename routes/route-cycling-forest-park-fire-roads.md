# Route: Forest Park Fire Roads Circuit (Moderate) 🟡

## Overview
| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Cycling |
| **Difficulty** | Moderate |
| **Distance** | ~15 miles |
| **Elevation Gain** | ~800 ft |
| **Terrain** | Gravel fire roads, some single-track sections |
| **Surface** | Packed gravel, dirt, some paved connectors |
| **Start** | Lower Macleay parking area (NW 29th Ave & NW Upshur St) |

## Description
A circuit through the heart of Forest Park using the fire road network. Routes combine Leif Erikson Drive with fire roads like Newberry Road and Lower Macleay Road. This is the go-to route for mountain bike training and building endurance — the gravel surface adds a stability challenge that's great for developing bike handling skills.

## Route Description
1. Start at Lower Macleay parking area (NW 29th Ave & NW Upshur St)
2. Head north on Leif Erikson Drive (paved, car-free)
3. At the intersection with Newberry Road, turn right onto the fire road
4. Follow Newberry Road north through the forest — wide gravel surface with canopy
5. Continue to the junction with Lower Macleay Road
6. Turn right on Lower Macleay Road and descend back to the start
7. Optional extension: Continue on fire roads to the St. Johns Bridge area for an extended loop

## Key Points
- **Lower Macleay Parking:** Main staging area — parking is free and usually available on weekdays
- **Newberry Road Fire Road:** The heart of the circuit — ~3 miles of packed gravel through old-growth forest
- **Lower Macleay Road:** Faster descent back to the trailhead
- **Fire Road Network:** Multiple connector routes allow you to customize the loop length
- **Forest Park Visitor Center:** Near the NW 30th Ave trailhead area

## Best For
- Mountain bike training
- Gravel cycling
- Trail endurance building
- Group rides (wide fire roads allow passing)

## Trail Conditions & Preparedness
| Condition | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Dry | Standard mountain bike or gravel bike works well |
| Wet/Muddy | Gravel roads become slippery — wider tires (35mm+) recommended |
| Post-rain | Some sections may have standing water — mid-range tires ideal |

### What to Bring
- **Water:** No water fountains on fire roads — carry at least 1.5L
- **Tires:** 35-50mm width for gravel conditions
- **Repair Kit:** Tubes, multi-tool, tire levers — remote from services
- **Layers:** Forest canopy is cooler — bring a wind vest
- **Emergency:** Phone, ID, trail map (cell service spotty)

## Cautions
- **No marked route:** The fire road network is interconnected — carry a map or download GPS track
- **Other users:** Shared with hikers and trail runners — slow down and announce passes
- **No street lights:** Do not ride after dark unless you have lights
- **Vehicle access:** Some fire roads are open to maintenance vehicles — stay right

## Seasonal Tips
- **Spring:** Muddy sections near Newberry Road — wait for trails to dry
- **Summer:** Best conditions — dry gravel, warm canopy-covered sections
- **Fall:** Beautiful foliage, potentially wet leaves on gravel
- **Winter:** Icy on elevated sections — studded tires recommended
