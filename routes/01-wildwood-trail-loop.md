# Wildwood Trail Loop — Running Route

**Category:** Running  
**Distance:** ~5 miles (forest park main section)  
**Difficulty:** Moderate  
**Elevation Gain:** ~800 ft  
**Surface:** Natural trail (roots, rocks, dirt)  
**Season:** Year-round (mud season Nov–May)

---

## Route Description

The Wildwood Trail is the crown jewel of Forest Park — a 30+ mile footpath that winds through dense old-growth forest. This route covers the main Park section from near the Washington Street trailhead down toward the Lower Macleay area (or reverse). The trail threads through towering Douglas fir and Western red cedar, with creek crossings, fern-covered ravines, and occasional wildlife sightings.

## Directions (Out & Back: Lower Macleay → Balanced Rock → Lower Macleay)

1. Start at Lower Macleay Trailhead Bridge (45.534, -122.713)
2. Follow Lower Macleay Trail northward through Macleay Park (1.0 mi)
3. Merge right onto **Wildwood Trail** in dense forest
4. Continue north on Wildwood Trail past **Ramona Falls junction**
5. Pass **Skunk cabbage** wet zones in spring
6. Reach **Balanced Rock** overlook area (45.571, -122.772) at ~2.5 mi — turnaround point
7. Return the same way to Lower Macleay

**Total:** ~5 miles (2.5 mi each way)  
**Estimated time:** 45–60 minutes

## Equipment

- Trail shoes recommended (rooted surface)
- Trekking poles for downhill on return
- Headlamp if you might run late (thick canopy = dark)
- Water (no fountains on Wildwood Trail)

## Highlights

- 🌲 Ancient forest canopy (some trees 400+ years old)
- 🦇 Winter bat colonies near rock overhangs
- 🌧️ Seasonal streams and moss-draped trees
- 🏵️ Balanced Rock geological formation
