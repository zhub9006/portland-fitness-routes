# Lower Macleay → Witch's Castle Walking Route

**Type:** Walking/Hiking
**OSM Verified Distance:** 3,712 m
**Estimated Duration:** ~45 min walking (5 km/h)
**Elevation Gain:** ~250 ft
**Difficulty:** Easy-Moderate

---

## Route Overview

A short but beautiful walk from Lower Macleay Park to the famous Witch's Castle (Stone House) ruins on the Wildwood Trail. This is one of the most popular short hikes in Forest Park and makes a great warm-up or family outing.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions (OSM Verified)

1. **Start:** Lower Macleay Park trailhead (45.534, -122.713)
2. Head west on **NW Lovejoy Street** (~138 m)
3. Turn left onto **Witch's Castle Trail / Lower Macleay Trail** (~3,414 m)
4. Navigate through switchbacks and creek crossings
5. **Arrive:** Witch's Castle (Stone House) ruins (45.528, -122.725)

## Seasonal Notes

- **Spring:** Lush green; beautiful moss on the stone ruins; wildflowers
- **Summer:** Dense canopy provides shade; creek may be low
- **Fall:** Spectacular foliage on the Witch's Castle approach
- **Winter:** Trail can be muddy and icy; waterproof footwear essential

## Tips

- The stone house is on private property; please leave no trace
- Kids love this hike; it's short enough for most ages
- Bring water even for this short route — the forest canopy blocks water fountains
- The trail has multiple creek crossings that may require stepping stones
- Extend the hike by continuing on Wildwood Trail to Pittock Mansion (add ~1.2 km)
