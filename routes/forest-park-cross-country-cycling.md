# Forest Park Cross-Country Cycling Route 🚴

**Route Type:** Cycling / Road Cycling
**OSM Verified Distances:**
- St. Johns Bridge → Downtown: **15,028 m** (9.3 miles)
- Forest Park Center → Downtown: **9,440 m** (5.9 miles)
- Downtown → Forest Park Center: **6,544 m** (4.1 miles)
**Estimated Duration:** ~2,013 sec (St. Johns to downtown) / ~756 sec (Forest Park → Downtown)
**Elevation Gain:** ~800 ft descent from St. Johns
**Difficulty:** Moderate
**Recommended Bike:** Road bike or hybrid

## Route Description

A cross-country cycling route crossing Forest Park via the iconic 1931 Saint Johns Bridge, descending from St. Johns through the northern edge of Forest Park into NW Portland and connecting to downtown. Great for scenic commuting or training rides with a thrilling downhill finish.

## Route Options

### Option 1: St. Johns Bridge → Downtown (Full Route)
**Distance:** 15,028 m | **Duration:** ~33 minutes

Route: Saint Johns Bridge → North Philadelphia Ave → North Lombard → North Portland Road → North Marine Drive → East Mill Plain → West Mill Plain → West Fourth Plain → NW Lower River Road → NW Gateway Ave → NW Harborside Dr → Downtown waterfront

### Option 2: Forest Park Center → Downtown (Shorter)
**Distance:** 9,440 m | **Duration:** ~12 minutes
Route: St. Helens Road → Burnside St → Naito Parkway

### Option 3: Downtown → Forest Park Center (Reverse/Climb)
**Distance:** 6,544 m | **Duration:** ~11 minutes
Route: NW Cornell Road, NW Lovejoy Street — **Great climb for intervals!**

## Start / End Point

**Start (Option 1):** St. Johns Bridge
- GPS: 45.593, -122.747
- Use east pedestrian/bike lane on the bridge

**End (Option 1):** Downtown Portland waterfront (Naito Parkway area)

## Route Highlights

- **Saint Johns Bridge** — Iconic 1931 suspension with dedicated bike/ped lanes
- **North Marine Drive** — Riverside cycling with Willamette River views
- **~800 ft descent** — Thrilling ride from St. Johns to the river
- **Downtown finish** — Extend into the city center

## Safety Tips

1. St. Johns Bridge has a dedicated bike lane — enjoy the views!
2. North Marine Drive has vehicle traffic — stay alert
3. Use lights and bright clothing
4. The return climb (Downtown → Forest Park) is a beast — pace yourself
5. Downtown approach has heavier traffic — use bike lanes

## Seasonal Notes

- Spring/Fall: Cool, comfortable cycling
- Summer: Warm descent, light clothing
- Winter: Icy bridge routes — studded tires recommended
