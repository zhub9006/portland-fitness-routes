# Route 1: Leif Erikson Drive Out-and-Back

## Overview
| Detail | Info |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Walking |
| **Difficulty** | Easy 🟢 |
| **Distance** | ~5 miles out-and-back (10 miles total) |
| **Elevation Gain** | ~400 ft |
| **Terrain** | Paved road, well-maintained |
| **Start** | Lower Macleay Park entrance (NW Cornell Rd & NW 29th Ave) |

## Description
Leif Erikson Drive is the main car-free road running through Forest Park. It's the most popular route for walkers and runners. This out-and-back covers the scenic lower section from Lower Macleay Park to the Hoyt Arboretum area.

## Key Points
- Wildwood Trail intersection
- Hoyt Arboretum
- Pittock Mansion view
- Cathedral Park (at the western end)

## OSM Routing Data
**Mode:** Foot

## Best Practices
- **Best For:** Beginners, casual walkers, dog walkers
- **When to Go:** Early morning (6-9 AM) or late afternoon
- **Car-Free Hours:** Weekdays dawn to ~10am, all day Sundays
- **Note:** Can be muddy after rain — waterproof shoes recommended

## Seasonal Notes
| Season | Conditions | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Spring | Wet trails, wildflowers | Waterproof shoes, bring layers |
| Summer | Dry | Early morning rides, hydrate |
