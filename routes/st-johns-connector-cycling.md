# St. Johns Bridge to Forest Park Cycling Route

- **Distance:** 15.0 mi (24.1 km) one way / 30 mi round trip
- **Elevation Gain:** ~400 ft
- **Estimated Time:** 60–75 min one way
- **Type:** Out-and-back
- **Terrain:** Paved roads, bridge, some city streets
- **Difficulty:** Moderate

## Description

A scenic route from St. Johns down through the bridge and into Forest Park. The St. Johns Bridge (built 1931) is one of Portland's most iconic structures — a steel-hulled suspension bridge spanning the Willamette River. Ride down through Cathedral Park, cross the bridge, and continue into the heart of Forest Park.

## Route Details

1. **St. Johns area** → North Oswego Avenue
2. **North Lombard Street** → North Richmond Avenue
3. **North Philadelphia Avenue** → St. Johns Bridge
4. **NW Bridge Avenue** → NW Germantown Road
5. **Forest Park center** / Wildwood Trail trailhead

## Notes
- **Bridge path:** The bridge has a shared-use path for cyclists and pedestrians
- **Can be busy:** The bridge path gets congested on weekends — go early
- **Forest Park extension:** Combine with Wildwood Trail or Ridge Loop for a full day ride
- **Views:** The bridge offers stunning views of the river, St. Johns neighborhood, and the city

## Getting There
- **Parking:** Cathedral Park (near bridge) or Forest Park Field House
- **Transit:** TriMet bus lines 4, 44, 72
