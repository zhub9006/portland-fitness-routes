# St. Johns Connector Cycling Route

A thrilling descent from St. Johns into Forest Park via the St. Johns Bridge. A gradient-fueled adventure.

## Route Overview

- **Type:** Cycling
- **Total Distance:** 14,749 m (St. Johns to Park)
- **Elevation Gain:** ~800 ft (descent from St. Johns)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Challenging (descent is fast!)
- **Surface:** Road; mix of bridge, neighborhoods, and park roads

---

## Route Description

This route starts in St. Johns on the east bank of the Willamette River and follows a scenic route south through residential neighborhoods, crossing the iconic St. Johns Bridge into Forest Park. The descent from St. Johns is exhilarating — this is a pure gradient thrill ride.

### Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start at St. Johns Bridge area** (45.593, -122.747)
   - East side of St. Johns

2. **Cross the St. Johns Bridge** (1,093 m)
   - Iconic suspension bridge — stunning views of the Willamette River and NW Portland
   - Watch for wind!

3. **Cross to NW Bridge Avenue** on the west side (958 m)
   - Descend into the NW neighborhoods

4. **Continue to NW Saint Helens Road** (191 m)
   - Transition to Forest Park corridor

5. **Head south/east through Forest Park via NW Saint Helens Road** (3,072 m)
   - Into the forest
   - Very scenic

6. **Continue through Forest Park interior** (3,072 m)
   - Deep forest sections
   - Wildlife corridor

7. **Complete the route at Forest Park destinations** (45.571, -122.772)
   - Various meetup points within the park

### Key Highlights
- 🌉 St. Johns Bridge — iconic Portland landmark with river views
- 📉 Fast descent — 800+ ft of gravity-powered speed!
- 🌲 Forest Park entry — arrive in the forest's heart
- 🏘️ Residential neighborhoods — peaceful mid-section
- ⚠️ Fast descent — require caution and good brakes!

### Preparation Tips
- **CRITICAL:** Check your brakes before the descent!
- Wear a helmet — the St. Johns Bridge is high and exposed
- Watch for wind on the bridge — it can push you
- The descent from bridge to park is ~2 km of fast riding
- Make sure your tires are properly inflated
