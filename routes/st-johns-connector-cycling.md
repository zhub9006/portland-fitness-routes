# St. Johns Bridge Connector Cycling Route

**Route Type:** Cycling | **Distance:** ~15.0 mi (24.1 km) one way | **Elevation Gain:** ~950 ft | **Difficulty:** Moderate to Hard

## Overview

A spectacular ride from the St. Johns Bridge down into Forest Park and through Portland's northwest neighborhoods. This route features the iconic 1931 St. Johns Bridge suspension crossing, followed by a fast descent through Forest Park's back roads into the city.

## Key Features
- **St. Johns Bridge:** The engineering marvel — ride across the 1931 suspension bridge with spectacular views
- **Cathedral Park:** Beautiful neighborhood under the bridge with green spaces
- **Descent:** The Bridge Avenue descent from the ridge into Forest Park is a rider favorite
- **Multi-use:** Extend the route east to downtown or continue north to the Pearl District

## Tips
- The St. Johns Bridge has a 15 mph speed limit and is shared with pedestrians — ride carefully
- The descent on Bridge Avenue is fast; check brakes before starting
- Weekend mornings are ideal — weekday traffic on Lombard and Richmond can be heavy
- Factor in 1.5–2 hours for the one-way route with stops
