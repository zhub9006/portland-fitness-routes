# Saint Johns Bridge to Forest Park Cycling Route

**Route Type:** 🚴 Cycling  
**Distance:** ~15.0 mi one way  
**Elevation Gain:** ~900 ft  
**Difficulty:** Moderate (hilly)  
**Surface:** Paved road  

## Overview

A scenic route from the St. Johns neighborhood through the iconic Saint Johns Bridge and into Forest Park. The descent through the NW hills offers stunning views of the Willamette River and Forest Park canopy.

## Route Details

| Segment | Distance | Key Features |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| St. Johns neighborhood → Saint Johns Bridge | ~6.1 mi (6,028 m) | North Lombard St, neighborhood streets | 
| Saint Johns Bridge → NW Bridge Ave | ~0.9 mi (854 m) | Cross the iconic 1931 suspension bridge | 
| NW Bridge Ave → NW Germantown Rd | ~2.0 mi (2,157 m) | Through Forest Park outer edges | 
| NW Germantown Rd → Forest Park Center | ~2.1 mi (2,071 m) | Descent to Forest Park heart | 
| NW Bridge Ave → NW Bridge Ave (full loop) | ~3.9 mi (3,929 m) | Via NW Saltzman Rd connection | 

### Turn-by-Turn Directions (OSM Verified)

1. From **St. Johns Bridge** (45.593, -122.747) — start on the bridge
2. Head south on **North Philadelphia Avenue** (168 m)
3. Continue on **North Ivanhoe Street** (324 m)
4. Connect to **North Richmond Avenue** (111 m)
5. Continue on **North Lombard Street** (466 m + 457 m)
6. Turn onto **North Smith Street** (267 m)
7. Continue on **North Columbia Way** (191 m)
8. Merge onto **North Midway Avenue** (356 m)
9. Connect to **NW Bridge Avenue** (174 m)
10. Turn onto **NW Germantown Road** (3,356 m)
11. Connect to **NW Kaiser Road** (739 m)
12. Continue to **NW Laidlaw Road** (500 m)
13. Return via **NW Bridge Avenue** and **NW Saltzman Road** to starting point

**Total distance:** ~15,028 m (~9.3 mi) one way

## Tips for Cyclists

- 🌉 **Bridge views:** Saint Johns Bridge offers incredible river and city views
- ⬇️ **Descent:** Fast downhill into NW Portland — brake check before corners
- 🚗 **Traffic:** Lombard Street can be busy; use the right lane
- 📍 **Carry water:** No water fountains on the bridge or route segments
