# Lower Macleay to Forest Park Core — Walking Route

**Category:** Walking  
**Distance:** ~6.5 miles one-way  
**Difficulty:** Easy-to-Moderate (gradual climb from Lower Macleay)  
**Duration:** ~88 minutes  
**Surface:** Natural trail (dirt, roots), some paved road sections

---

## Route Overview

This walk connects Portland's **Lower Macleay** neighborhood (east side of the West Hills) to **Forest Park's interior** in a single long, flowing continuous route. It combines urban-side trail corridors through the Macleay Park system with the wild forest expansiveness of Leif Erikson Drive — the famous "tree tunnel." perfect for a full-morning hike or an afternoon walking adventure.

---

## Direction Details

### Leg 1: Neighborhood + Lower Park (1.5 mi)
1. Start at **Lower Macleay Trailhead Bridge** (45.534, -122.713) — the iconic covered bridge entrance
2. Walk across the bridge into **Macleay Park**
3. Continue north on **Northwest 31st Avenue** (brief road walk) (~0.2 mi)
4. Cut through residential streets to collect **Northwest Vaughn Street** (residential, gentle climb)
5. Connect to **Leif Erikson Drive** via the signed trail-access path near upscale West Hills homes

### Leg 2: The Tunnel Walk (3.0 mi) — SHARED USE
6. From the southern access, ascend **Northwest Saint Helens Road** or parallel trail (~1.7 mi)
7. Arrive at the famous Leif Erikson Drive "tree tunnel" — verify it's closed to cars before walking!
8. Walk through the dense **canopy tunnel** — moss-covered fir and cedar coat every surface
9. Pass **Henderson Creek** — listen for wrentit and other songbirds
10. Scan for **Banana slugs** on the trail after rainy days (the park's unofficial mascot!)

### Leg 3: Forest Heart (1.5 mi)
11. Transition from Leif Erikson Drive onto the **Wildwood Trail** system
12. Walk through forest cathedral with 200+ ft canopy trees
13. Encounter the **Ramona Falls junction** (if continuing further east — popular side quest!)
14. End at the **Forest Park Nature Center** or Balanced Rock area (45.571, -122.772) for a natural turnaround

## Elevation

- **Start elevation:** ~200 ft (Lower Macleay near river level)
- **End elevation:** ~700 ft (Forest Park interior ridgeline)
- **Total gain:** ~500 ft spread over distance — gentle but continuous

## Seasonal Notes

- 🌸 **Spring (March–May):** Best for wildflowers and creek flow
- 🍃 **Summer (June–Aug):** Maximum shade; warm and dry
- 🍂 **Fall (Sept–Nov):** Colorful understory, lower water levels
- ❄️ **Winter (Dec–Feb):** Mosses glow in the rain; slippery but beautiful
- 🌧️ **Mud season** (Dec–Apr): Wear waterproof boots — trail gets muddy

---

## Packing List (Walking)

- 💧 Water (at least 1.5 L — no fountains past Macleay)
- 🗺️ Trail app or downloaded offline map (limited cell reception in the park)
- 🧥 Layer for temperature changes (5–10°F cooler inside the forest canopy)
- 🐕 Leash for dogs (required in all Portland parks)
- ☀️ Sunscreen + hat (surprisingly exposed on the Saint Helens road sections)
