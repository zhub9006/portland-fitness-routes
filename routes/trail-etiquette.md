# Trail Etiquette & Safety

Guidelines for responsible use of Forest Park trails.

---

## General Etiquette

- **Yield to pedestrians** — cyclists give way to walkers and runners
- **Keep right, pass on the left** — announce your presence with a bell or "On your left!"
- **Control your speed** — especially on downhill sections and blind corners
- **Stay on marked trails** — no cutting switchbacks or creating new paths
- **Pack it in, pack it out** — carry out all trash and waste

## Dog Walkers

- **Leash required** in Forest Park (Portland city ordinance)
- **Voice control** is an alternative where leashing is impractical
- **Clean up after your dog** — bring bags and dispose properly

## Seasonal Safety

### Spring
- Trails are wet and muddy — use waterproof footwear
- Watch for slippery roots and leaves

### Summer
- Trails dry out but can become hard and dusty
- Carry extra water — few water sources on trails
- Watch for ticks in grassy areas

### Fall
- Beautiful foliage but falling leaves obscure trail hazards
- Wet leaves on pavement can be very slippery
- Shorter daylight hours — bring a headlamp

### Winter
- Rain is frequent; ice on elevated sections
- Studded tires for bikes
- Reflective/hi-vis clothing — days are short
- Check trail conditions before heading out

## Wildlife Awareness

- **Deer:** Common at dawn and dusk — give space
- **Cougars:** Rare but possible — stay alert, don't run if spotted
- **Bears:** Very rare in the park but possible in late fall
- **Snakes:** Garter snakes are common in warm months — harmless but watch your step
