## Description

The iconic Leif Erikson Drive runs the full length of Forest Park, offering a scenic, car-free (morning and Sunday) cycling experience through one of the largest urban forests in the United States. The road is wide, well-paved, and shaded by towering old-growth canopy for most of its length. Starting from the central section near NW Saint Helens Road, this route takes you northward through dense forest with occasional vistas of the city skyline. A must-ride for any Portland cycling club member.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start** — Northwest Leif Erikson Drive near NW Saint Helens Road intersection (45.5495, -122.7339)
2. **Head north** on Leif Erikson Drive — the road starts gently rolling uphill
3. **Continue** through dense old-growth forest on Leif Erikson Drive — ~3.6 km of shaded canopy
4. **Pass** the NW Bridge Avenue intersection — a major cross-street with parking access
5. **Continue** north on Leif Erikson Drive — now climbing more noticeably with city views to the west
6. **Pass** the NW Germantown Road intersection — near Skyline Tavern (popular post-ride spot)
7. **Continue** to the northern terminus near NW Saltzman Road (45.5819, -122.7861)
8. **Turn around** and return south via the same route, or connect to NW Germantown Road for a different return path

## Return Option

- **Via Germantown Road:** At the north end, turn west on NW Germantown Road (~1.7 km) to reconnect with the southern section of Leif Erikson Drive for a longer loop (~11.3 km total)

## Amenities Nearby

- Trailhead parking at NW Bridge Avenue
- Parking near NW Germantown Road / Skyline Tavern area
- Skyline Tavern — post-ride food and drinks
- No water fountains on Leif Erikson Drive — bring your own

## Tips

- **Car-free hours:** Weekday mornings ~dawn to 10am, all day Sundays — best times to ride
- The road has some automotive traffic during peak hours — use caution and bell/pedal signals
- The road is shaded most of the year but can be icy in winter mornings
- Best training route for climbing — the north section has the steepest grades
- Great route for group rides — the road width allows side-by-side cycling
- Watch for trail runners and hikers on the roadside trail
