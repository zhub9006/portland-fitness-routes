## Description

A short but scenic cycling route from the Lower Macleay Park area into the Forest Park interior. This connector is perfect for cyclists who want to reach Forest Park's trail network from the northwest Portland neighborhoods. The route combines quiet neighborhood streets with a bike lane on NW 25th Ave, then transitions to paved trail entering the park.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start** — Lower Macleay Park Trailhead parking area (NW Upshur St & NW 28th Place, 45.5360, -122.7127)
2. **Head northwest** on NW Upshur St for ~150 m, turning right (north) onto NW 28th Ave
3. **Cycle** north on NW 28th Ave for ~150 m, then turn left (west) onto NW Savier St
4. **Follow** NW Savier St northwest for ~470 m — a quiet residential street with good bike accommodation
5. **Turn right** (north) onto NW 25th Ave — use the bike lane for ~310 m
6. **Continue** north on NW 25th Ave, crossing NW Lovejoy St (keep in bike lane)
7. **Continue** on NW 25th Ave for ~75 m, then turn left (west) onto NW Lovejoy St
8. **Cycle** west on NW Lovejoy St for ~140 m, arriving at NW Cornell Road
9. **Turn right** (north) onto NW Cornell Road — the road has a wide shoulder and becomes partially wooded
10. **Follow** NW Cornell Road north for ~600 m as it enters the Forest Park boundary — the road surface transitions to packed gravel
11. **Arrive** at the Forest Park interior trail network — connect to the main park trails for further exploration

## Return Option

- Retrace the same route back to Lower Macleay (~3.0 km)
- Or continue further north on NW Cornell Road to connect with the Qutherefore Lane exit for a different return path (~4.0 km total loop)

## Amenities Nearby

- Parking at Lower Macleay Park Trailhead
- The Anchor café on NW Germantown Road (near the route midpoint)
- No water in Forest Park interior — bring your own

## Tips

- The NW 25th Ave bike lane is popular with commuters — be predictable and signal turns
- NW Cornell Road into the park is a popular gravel/mulch surface — MTB bikes or hybrid bikes recommended
- The route is relatively flat with one short climb on NW Savier St
- Watch for pedestrians on the shared-use paths inside Forest Park
- Best in morning — the park interior can be foggy in the afternoon
- Sunday mornings are car-free on Leif Erikson Drive if you want to extend the ride
