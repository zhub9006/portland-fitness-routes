# portland-fitness-routes

A collection of walking, cycling, and running routes around **Forest Park** in Portland for our running club.

---

## 📍 Forest Park Overview

Forest Park is one of the largest urban forests in the United States, spanning over 5,200 acres with more than 80 miles of trails. Located in the West Hills of Portland, Oregon, it offers some of the best fitness routes in the Pacific Northwest.

**Main coordinates:** 45.571, -122.772

---

## 🏃 Running Routes

### Wildwood Trail Loop
- **Distance:** ~5 miles (forest park section: Washington Street to Lower Macleay)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Type:** Out & Back / Loop
- **Route:** Washington St trailhead → Wildwood Trail (dense forest canopy, some elevation)
- **Highlights:** Ancient ferns, towering Douglas fir, creek crossings
- **Coordinates:** Starts at 45.571, -122.772; follows the Wildwood Trail corridor (45.543, -122.738 to 45.581, -122.790)

---

## 🚶 Walking Routes

### 1. Lower Macleay to Forest Park Heart (Walking)
- **Distance:** ~6.5 miles
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (beginner-friendly with gradual climb)
- **Duration:** ~88 minutes
- **Route:** Lower Macleay Trail → Northwest Leif Erikson Drive (narrow section) → Forest Park core
- **Road Sections:** Northwest 31st Avenue, Northwest Vaughn Street, **Northwest Saint Helens Road** (key arterial through the park), Leif Erikson Drive
- **Highlights:** Historic Upper Macleay Park, beautiful fern-lined creek beds on the Leif Erikson stretch, old-growth forest vibes
- **Start:** 45.534, -122.713 (Lower Macleay)
- **End:** 45.571, -122.772 (Forest Park center)

### 2. Wildwood Ridge Trail Walk (Walking)
- **Distance:** ~3.3 miles (one-way from Forest Park center to Wildwood Road)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate-to-Strenuous (steady uphill + ridge exposure)
- **Duration:** ~64 minutes
- **Route:** Forest Park center → **Northwest Saint Helens Road** (1.7 mi) → **Northwest Bridge Avenue** → **Northwest Germantown Road** (3.4 mi) → **Northwest Skyline Boulevard** → Northwest College Drive
- **Highlights:** Panoramic ridge-line views of the Portland metro, Washington Park, Mt. Hood on clear days, wildflower meadows in spring
- **Start:** 45.571, -122.772
- **End:** 45.581, -122.790 (Wildwood Trail area)

### 3. Forest Park East-to-West Stroll (Walking)
- **Distance:** ~3.3 miles
- **Difficulty:** Easy-to-Moderate
- **Duration:** ~65 minutes
- **Route:** Thunder Crest area → **Northwest Skyline Boulevard** (2.5 mi) through ridge forest → West Hills residential area
- **Highlights:** Quiet neighborhood streets, deep forest canopy, hilltop views
- **Start:** 45.555, -122.769
- **End:** 45.570, -122.772

## 🚴 Cycling Routes

### 1. Forest Park Skyline Cycling Route 🌟
- **Distance:** ~12.2 miles
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (skilled bike handling on narrow ridge roads)
- **Duration:** ~19 minutes at avg pace / ~21 min actual
- **Route:** Start on **Northwest 53rd Drive** → **Northwest Thompson Road** → **Northwest Skyline Boulevard** (6 mi, the scenic spine!) → **Northwest Germantown Road** (3.3 mi) → cross to **West Burnside Road** → **Southwest Barnes Road** → **Southwest Leahy Road** → **Southwest Taylor Street**
- **Highlights:** Sweeping ridge views, multiple panoramic viewpoints, car-free sections on Skyline, natural beauty
- **Best:** Weekday mornings; avoid weekends when crowded
- **Start/End:** Forest Park center area (45.575, -122.760)

### 2. Leif Erikson Drive Cycling Loop
- **Distance:** ~4.2 miles
- **Difficulty:** Easy (car-free path, great for beginners and families)
- **Duration:** ~6 minutes at avg pace / ~35 min ride
- **Route:** Trailhead parking → **Northwest Leif Erikson Drive** (one-way car-free loop through old-growth forest) → return via same path
- **Road Sections:** Northwest Saint Helens Road (key access) → Leif Erikson Drive (tree tunnel!)
- **Highlights:** Tunnels of old-growth trees, creekside riding, fern canopy overhead, wildlife spotting
- **Start:** 45.549, -122.734 (Leif Erikson & Saint Helens junction)
- **Note:** Leif Erikson Drive is closed to vehicles certain hours/days — verify before riding!

### 3. Forest Park to Willamette Valley Cycling Route
- **Distance:** ~14.6 miles
- **Difficulty:** Moderate (long downhill on Leif Erikson / Saint Helens, careful on return)
- **Duration:** ~21 minutes at avg pace / ~5 min actual; plan 1-2 hours round trip with rest
- **Route:** Forest Park center → down via **Northwest Saint Helens Road** → **West Burnside Road** (2.5 mi) → city streets (Burnside) → Willamette River waterfront
- **Highlights:** Dramatic descent through forest, bonus time in the city core, reward with waterfront snack
- **Start:** 45.575, -122.760
- **End:** Downtown Portland (~45.520, -122.680) — extend as you like!
- **⚠️ Warning:** Return ride is up! Consider driving back or take it easy.

### 4. Saltzman Road Warm-Up Loop (Cycling)
- **Distance:** ~2.1 miles
- **Difficulty:** Easy (short, flat-ish warm-up)
- **Duration:** ~3 minutes at avg pace
- **Route:** Forest Park northern edge → **Northwest Saint Helens Road** (1.6 mi) → **Northwest Saltzman Road** (0.5 mi)
- **Best:** Great for warming up before a longer ride or running
- **Start/End:** Forest Park north entrance area (45.575, -122.760)

## ⚠️ Safety Reminders

- **Bear right** on shared trails (hikers have right-of-way on Wildwood)
- **Leash required** for dogs on all Portland parks trails
- **Trail conditions** can be muddy Nov–May — bring traction for shoes/bikes
- **Visibility** — leash-up wildlife (coyotes, deer, banana slugs!) may appear
- **Car-free hours** — Leif Erikson Drive closes to cars Mon/Tue/Thu all day, Sat after noon; always verify
