## Description

A beautiful out-and-back route starting from the Lower Macleay Trailhead at the edge of the city, following the paved trail through lush old-growth forest up to the historic Stone House (Witch's Castle) on the Wildwood Trail. The trail climbs gently through towering Douglas firs and western red cedars, crossing several small streams along the way. Perfect for a group warm-up or a standalone nature walk.

## Turn-by-Turn Directions

1. **Start** — Lower Macleay Park Trailhead (NW Upshur St & NW 28th Place)
2. **Head northwest** on the Lower Macleay Trail (paved, well-maintained)
3. **Continue** on Northwest Upshur Street for ~278 m
4. **Turn onto** the Wildwood Trail at the trail junction (packed dirt, single-track)
5. **Follow** the Wildwood Trail northbound through the forest — ~2.4 km through towering old-growth trees
6. **Arrive** at the Stone House (Witch's Castle) — a historic stone structure built in the 1800s, great photo spot
7. **Turn around** and retrace your steps back to the trailhead

## Amenities Nearby

- Parking at Lower Macleay Trailhead (NW 28th Place)
- Restrooms at Macleay Park entrance
- No water on trail — bring your own
- Nearby: The Anchor café on NW Germantown Road

## Tips

- Trail can be muddy after rain — wear trail-appropriate shoes
- The Stone House is a popular stop — allow extra time for photos
- Early morning is best to avoid afternoon fog in the forest
- Watch for trail crossings and creek intersections
- Leashed dogs are permitted on this trail
